# The Gospel of Thomas

## Overview

This work is a contemplative devotional based on the 114 sayings found in the Gospel of Thomas.

Rather than approaching the text as a work of historical analysis or theological debate, this project invites readers to encounter the sayings as opportunities for reflection, awareness, and inner transformation. Each entry explores a single saying through the lens of contemplative spirituality, drawing attention to themes of presence, awakening, self-knowledge, wholeness, and Divine Love.

The result is a collection designed to be read slowly—perhaps with a morning cup of coffee, during a quiet walk, or in a few moments of silence before the day begins.

## About the Gospel of Thomas

The Gospel of Thomas is an early Christian text discovered among the Nag Hammadi manuscripts in Egypt in 1945. Unlike the canonical gospels, it contains no narrative of Jesus' life, death, or resurrection. Instead, it presents a collection of 114 sayings attributed to Jesus.

Scholars continue to debate its origins and dating. Some believe portions of the text may preserve very early traditions, while others see it as a later expression of mystical or gnostic Christianity. Regardless of its precise origins, the text has intrigued spiritual seekers for generations because of its emphasis on direct experience, inner awakening, and discovering the kingdom already present.

Many of its sayings echo themes found in the canonical gospels:

* The kingdom of God
* Self-discovery
* Spiritual awakening
* Seeing with new eyes
* Inner transformation
* The hidden treasure of divine reality

## Why This Project?

I first encountered the Gospel of Thomas after years of hearing that it should be avoided.

Growing up in an evangelical environment, non-canonical writings were often dismissed as false or dangerous. Over time, however, I came to appreciate that the early Christian movement contained many different communities wrestling with the meaning of Jesus and his teachings.

The Gospel of Thomas may not have become part of the New Testament canon, but that does not mean it lacks spiritual value. Like many ancient texts, it can still challenge, inspire, and deepen one's understanding of the inner life.

This devotional is an attempt to sit with these sayings patiently, allowing them to serve as companions on the contemplative path rather than problems to be solved.

## What You'll Find

The book contains 114 daily readings.

Each entry includes:

* A title inspired by the saying
* The saying itself
* A contemplative reflection
* A simple breath prayer

The reflections intentionally avoid doctrinal arguments and scholarly debates. Instead, they focus on themes such as:

* Awareness
* Presence
* The kingdom within and around us
* Healing inner divisions
* Wholeness and integration
* Trusting the spiritual journey
* Discovering the sacred in ordinary life

## A Contemplative Approach

The Gospel of Thomas often speaks in paradoxes, riddles, and symbolic images.

Rather than forcing a single interpretation, this devotional embraces mystery where mystery remains. The goal is not certainty but attentiveness. The sayings become invitations to pause, reflect, and listen more deeply.

Again and again, Thomas points toward a simple but profound possibility:

> The kingdom is already here.

The challenge is not finding it.

The challenge is learning to see it.

## Reading Slowly

This is not a book intended to be rushed through.

Read one entry at a time.

Pause after each reflection.

Sit quietly with the breath prayer.

Allow the sayings to unfold gradually over days and weeks.

You may discover that the deepest insights arrive not while seeking answers, but while resting in attentive presence.
