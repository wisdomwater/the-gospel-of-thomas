## 52 - The One Before You

> *His disciples said to him, "Twenty-four prophets spoke in Israel, and all of them spoke in you."*
>
> *He said to them, "You have omitted the one living in your presence and have spoken (only) of the dead."*

The disciples honor the prophets of the past. They recognize wisdom that has been handed down through generations and see its echoes in Jesus. There is beauty in this. We all inherit treasures from those who came before us—teachers, saints, mentors, and loved ones whose words continue to guide our lives.

Yet Jesus gently redirects their attention. While they are speaking of those who once lived, they overlook the living presence standing before them. Their eyes are turned toward what was, while they miss what is.

This is a temptation many of us know. We can become fascinated with yesterday's insights and tomorrow's hopes while remaining absent to the gift of the present moment. We remember past experiences of God. We read the words of wise teachers. We anticipate future breakthroughs. Yet all the while, life is unfolding right here, waiting to be noticed.

The living presence of God is never confined to history. The same Spirit that inspired prophets and sages continues to breathe through this moment. Wisdom is not merely something preserved in sacred texts or cherished memories. It is alive. It speaks through the ordinary events of today, through quiet intuition, through the beauty of creation, through the gentle movements of the heart.

Contemplative practice trains us to recognize this living reality. Instead of constantly looking elsewhere, we learn to become present. We discover that the sacred is not only found in what was revealed long ago. It is also revealed in the conversation before us, the breath within us, the person beside us, and the silence surrounding us.

Perhaps Jesus is inviting us to honor the past without becoming trapped in it. The prophets have their place. The great teachers have their place. But their deepest purpose may be to help us awaken to the living presence available now. The finger points toward the moon; eventually, we must lift our eyes and see the moon itself.

Today, notice where your attention is dwelling. Are you lost in memories, regrets, plans, or expectations? Gently return to what is here. The sacred is not absent. The living presence you seek may be closer than your next breath.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Here and now*
>
> Exhale: *I welcome Your presence*
