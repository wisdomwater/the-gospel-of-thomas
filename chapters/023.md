## 23 - Standing as One

> *Jesus said, "I shall choose you, one out of a thousand, and two out of ten thousand, and they shall stand as a single one."*

This saying speaks with the language of rarity and unity. At first, our attention may be drawn to the numbers, to the image of a few being chosen from among many. Yet the saying ends with a different emphasis. Those who are gathered do not stand apart in superiority or distinction. They "stand as a single one."

In ordinary life, we are constantly encouraged to see ourselves as separate. We compare ourselves to others, measure our worth, and construct identities based on success, failure, status, or belonging. The ego thrives on distinction. It wants to know how it ranks and where it stands. But the deeper spiritual journey often moves in the opposite direction.

As we awaken, we begin to discover that what is most essential within us is not separate from what is most essential within anyone else. Beneath the countless differences that make each person unique lies a shared life, a shared source, a shared belonging. The deeper we travel into our true selves, the more we encounter this hidden unity.

Perhaps the "choosing" described here is not about exclusion but about responsiveness. In every generation there are those who become willing to listen deeply, to awaken from distraction, and to follow the quiet call of the heart. They are not necessarily more gifted or more worthy than others. They have simply become available to what has always been calling them.

The beautiful paradox is that the journey toward authentic individuality eventually leads to communion. As we let go of false identities, we do not become less ourselves. We become more fully ourselves. Yet in that authenticity, we discover an unexpected kinship with all life. The walls that once separated us begin to soften.

To stand as a single one is not to lose your uniqueness. It is to discover the unity that exists beneath all uniqueness. Like many branches drawing life from a single root, or many waves arising from a single sea, we remain distinct while sharing a common source.

Today, consider what divisions you may be carrying—between yourself and others, between yourself and God, even within your own heart. Beneath those divisions there is a deeper reality waiting to be remembered. The sacred path does not end in isolation. It leads toward a unity spacious enough to hold us all.
