## 53 - The Heart's Transformation

> *His disciples said to him, "Is circumcision beneficial or not?"*
>
> *He said to them, "If it were beneficial, their father would beget them already circumcised from their mother. Rather, the true circumcision in spirit has become completely profitable."*

The disciples ask a question about an outward religious practice. Jesus responds by turning their attention inward. Rather than focusing on what can be seen from the outside, he points toward a transformation that takes place within the spirit.

Throughout life, we are often drawn to visible markers of identity. We seek signs that tell us who belongs, who is faithful, who is growing, and who is not. Outward practices can have value. They can remind us of deeper truths and help shape our lives. Yet Jesus seems to suggest that no external sign can substitute for the work of the heart.

The phrase *"circumcision in spirit"* evokes an image of removing what hinders life. Just as a gardener prunes away what no longer serves the growth of a plant, the spiritual journey often involves letting go of attitudes, fears, attachments, and illusions that keep us from living fully. Something unnecessary is gently cut away so that something deeper can flourish.

This kind of transformation cannot be accomplished merely by following a rule or adopting a label. It unfolds through awareness, honesty, and openness to grace. We begin to notice the ways we protect ourselves from love. We recognize the stories that keep us separate from others. We become willing to release what no longer aligns with the deepest truth of who we are.

Contemplative practice is, in many ways, a participation in this inner work. Each time we sit in silence, each time we return to presence, each time we choose compassion over judgment, something within us is being softened and refined. The heart becomes more spacious. The spirit becomes more free.

Perhaps Jesus is reminding us that what matters most is not the mark we carry on the outside but the transformation occurring within. The sign of spiritual growth is not found in appearances. It is found in a heart becoming more loving, more open, more awake to the presence of God in all things.

Today, notice what may be asking to be released from your life. Not with force or self-condemnation, but with gentleness. Trust that every letting go creates room for something more alive. The work of the spirit is not to diminish you, but to reveal the deeper self that has been there all along.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Soften my heart*
>
> Exhale: *Reveal what is true*
