## 106 - Making the Two One

> *Jesus said, "When you make the two one, you will become the sons of man, and when you say, 'Mountain, move away,' it will move away."*

Throughout our lives, we experience ourselves as divided. We divide the sacred from the ordinary, the spiritual from the physical, the inner life from the outer life. We divide who we are from who we think we should be. Much of our suffering grows in the space between these imagined separations.

Jesus speaks here of making the two one. He does not explain exactly what the two are, and perhaps that is part of the gift. Each of us carries our own divisions. We know the places where we feel torn between competing desires, conflicting identities, or opposing voices within ourselves. The saying invites us to notice these fractures and gently move toward wholeness.

This kind of unity is not achieved by force. It does not require eliminating one side in favor of another. Rather, it emerges through reconciliation. We begin to welcome what we once rejected. We allow light to reach the hidden places. We discover that healing often comes not through winning an inner battle, but through embracing a deeper harmony.

As this integration unfolds, something remarkable happens. Energy once consumed by conflict becomes available for love, creativity, and presence. We become less fragmented and more rooted. We act from a deeper center rather than being pulled in a dozen directions at once. The self grows simpler, not because life becomes easier, but because we are no longer at war within.

The image of the mountain follows naturally. Mountains often appear immovable. They represent obstacles, fears, habits, and burdens that seem larger than our ability to overcome them. Yet when we live from a place of inner unity, we discover a strength that was hidden by our division. What once appeared impossible begins to shift.

Perhaps the mountain does not move because we have gained control over the world. Perhaps it moves because we have become aligned with a deeper reality. When the heart is gathered into wholeness, barriers lose some of their power. New possibilities emerge. The path forward becomes visible where before there seemed to be none.

Today, consider where you feel divided. Is there a part of yourself waiting to be welcomed rather than resisted? Is there an old separation that no longer serves you? The invitation is not to strive for perfection, but to move gently toward integration. In that movement, the seemingly immovable may begin to yield.
