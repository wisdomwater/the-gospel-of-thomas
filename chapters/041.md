## 41 - What We Are Willing to Receive

> *Jesus said, "Whoever has something in his hand will receive more, and whoever has nothing will be deprived of even the little he has."*

At first hearing, this saying can feel troubling. It seems to suggest that those who already have will receive more, while those with little will lose even that. Yet as with many of Jesus' sayings, there may be a deeper invitation hidden beneath the surface of the words.

Consider what it means to have something "in your hand." A hand can only receive what it is open to hold. Spiritually speaking, this may point to the qualities of attention, gratitude, awareness, and receptivity. Those who recognize and treasure the gifts already present in their lives often become capable of receiving even more. Their awareness expands, and with it their capacity for wonder.

The opposite can also be true. When we move through life unaware of the gifts around us, we gradually lose touch with them. Beauty goes unnoticed. Opportunities pass unseen. Wisdom arrives but finds no welcome. The blessing itself may still be present, yet it slips from our experience because we are not truly receiving it.

There is a subtle law of the soul at work here. Gratitude deepens gratitude. Awareness deepens awareness. Love deepens love. The more we practice noticing, the more there is to notice. The more we open ourselves to the sacred, the more the sacred seems to appear everywhere. What was once hidden becomes visible.

This saying may also speak to spiritual growth itself. Every insight we receive carries a responsibility. Wisdom is not merely something to admire; it is something to live. When we embody what we have learned, deeper understanding often follows. But when we ignore what has been given, even the little understanding we possess can fade into memory.

The good news is that "having" in this sense is not about wealth, status, or achievement. It is about openness. A person with a humble and receptive heart may possess more spiritual richness than someone with every outward advantage. The kingdom often begins with a single seed of awareness, faithfully tended.

Today, take inventory not of your possessions, but of your blessings. Notice the breath moving through your lungs, the people who have touched your life, the moments of beauty that surround you, and the wisdom already present within your experience. Hold them gently in your hands. What is received with gratitude has a way of multiplying.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Open my hands to receive*
>
> Exhale: *Open my eyes to abundance*
