## 18 - Returning to the Beginning

> *The disciples said to Jesus, "Tell us how our end will be." Jesus said, "Have you discovered, then, the beginning, that you look for the end? For where the beginning is, there will the end be. Blessed is he who will take his place in the beginning; he will know the end and will not experience death."*

Like the disciples, we often want to know how things will end. We wonder about the future, about what lies beyond this life, about where our journey is leading. The mind naturally reaches ahead, searching for certainty. Yet Jesus responds with a surprising question. Instead of speaking about the end, he points them back to the beginning.

There is wisdom in this redirection. So much of our anxiety comes from trying to live in a future that has not yet arrived. We imagine outcomes, rehearse possibilities, and seek answers to questions that remain hidden. Yet the deepest truths are rarely found by racing ahead. They are found by returning to what is already here.

What is this beginning? Perhaps it is the simple awareness that precedes all our striving. Perhaps it is the sacred presence that existed before our accomplishments, failures, identities, and stories. Before we became entangled in the countless roles we play, there was a deeper life quietly flowing within us. The beginning is not merely something behind us in time. It is something available to us now.

When we return to this deeper center, we begin to see life differently. The frantic need to control the future softens. We discover that the same Presence that carried us into this moment will carry us into the next. The beginning and the end are not opposites. They are held together within a reality larger than our understanding.

The saying's final promise speaks of one who "will not experience death." Contemplatively, this may point toward the discovery of a life that cannot be reduced to beginnings and endings. Everything in the visible world changes form. Seasons pass. Bodies age. Circumstances shift. Yet beneath these changes is something enduring—a life rooted in the eternal presence of God.

Today, rather than reaching toward the horizon, return to the beginning. Return to this breath. Return to this moment. Return to the quiet awareness beneath your thoughts. There, in the place where all journeys begin, you may find that the end has already been embraced by a love that never leaves.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Return me to the beginning*
>
> Exhale: *Where Your presence remains*
