## 77 - The Light in All Things

> *Jesus said, "It is I who am the light which is above them all. It is I who am the all. From me did the all come forth, and unto me did the all extend. Split a piece of wood, and I am there. Lift up the stone, and you will find me there."*

This saying opens with a vision of immense grandeur. The light Jesus speaks of is not confined to a temple, a nation, or a particular moment in history. It is the light from which all things arise and toward which all things move. It is a light so vast that nothing exists outside its reach.

Yet the saying does not remain in the heights. After speaking of the all, Jesus points to ordinary things: a piece of wood, a stone resting on the ground. The infinite and the everyday are brought together. The sacred is not only found in mountaintop experiences or moments of extraordinary insight. It is woven into the fabric of ordinary existence.

Many of us live as though God is somewhere else—hidden behind the next achievement, the next spiritual breakthrough, or the next season of life. We search for the sacred in distant places while overlooking the miracle already present before us. This saying gently challenges that habit. What if the Divine is not absent from the world but shining through it?

The contemplative path often involves learning to see again. Not seeing new things, but seeing familiar things with new eyes. The rustling leaves, the warmth of sunlight, the face of a stranger, the sound of rain against a window—all become invitations to awareness. The world begins to appear less as a collection of separate objects and more as a living expression of a deeper Presence.

This does not mean that every object is God, but that nothing is abandoned by God. The same light that shines in moments of prayer also shines through the ordinary moments we are tempted to dismiss. The Divine Presence is not waiting at the end of the journey. It is quietly meeting us in the midst of it.

Perhaps that is the invitation hidden in these words. Before rushing past this moment in search of another, pause. Notice what is here. The stone beneath your feet, the breath in your lungs, the life unfolding around you. The light has not withdrawn from creation. It is still shining, waiting to be recognized.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Your light fills all things*
>
> Exhale: *Open my eyes to see*
