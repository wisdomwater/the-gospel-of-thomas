## 82 - Near the Fire

> *Jesus said, "He who is near me is near the fire, and he who is far from me is far from the kingdom."*

There is warmth in a fire, but there is also transformation. Fire gives light in the darkness. It provides comfort on a cold night. Yet fire also changes whatever it touches. Wood becomes flame. What was rigid becomes ash. Nothing comes close to fire and remains entirely the same.

Jesus says that those who are near him are near the fire. This is a striking image, for the spiritual life is often imagined as a path of comfort and reassurance alone. Yet genuine encounter with the Divine is more than consolation. It illuminates. It reveals. It gently burns away what is false so that what is true can emerge more clearly.

Many of us long for the Kingdom while hoping to avoid transformation. We want peace without surrender, wisdom without humility, and awakening without disruption. Yet the fire of God's presence works differently. It does not come to condemn us but to refine us. It invites us into greater honesty, deeper love, and a more authentic way of being.

The fire may appear in unexpected forms. It may arrive as a difficult conversation that exposes an old wound. It may come as a moment of silence that reveals how restless the mind has become. It may emerge as a growing awareness that certain patterns no longer serve the life we are being called to live. Though uncomfortable, these moments often become the very places where grace is at work.

To be far from this fire is not necessarily to be abandoned. It may simply mean remaining distant from the transforming presence that leads us into wholeness. We can spend years circling around spiritual ideas while keeping the deeper movements of the heart untouched. The Kingdom remains near, but we hesitate at the edge of its flame.

Perhaps the invitation of this saying is not merely to admire the fire but to draw closer. Trust that the light which reveals is also the light that heals. Trust that the warmth which softens is also the warmth that renews. The Kingdom is not only a destination ahead of us. It is the living reality that begins to unfold whenever we step closer to the fire of Divine Love.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Draw me near to Your fire*
>
> Exhale: *Transform me in love*
