## 56 - Beyond the Surface of Things

> *Jesus said, "Whoever has come to understand the world has found (only) a corpse, and whoever has found a corpse is superior to the world."*

This saying carries a stark image. Jesus speaks of understanding the world and discovering a corpse. At first, the words may seem dark or unsettling. Yet perhaps they point toward a profound awakening: the realization that what we often chase and cling to cannot ultimately satisfy the deepest longings of the soul.

Many of us spend years seeking fulfillment in the world's promises. We pursue success, recognition, possessions, certainty, or control. None of these things are inherently wrong. Yet sooner or later, we discover their limitations. What once seemed capable of completing us reveals itself to be temporary. The excitement fades. The achievement passes. The applause grows quiet.

This realization can feel disappointing at first. Yet it is often the beginning of wisdom. To find the "corpse" is to see clearly that what is passing cannot become our ultimate foundation. It is to awaken from the illusion that lasting peace can be built upon things that continually change.

The contemplative path invites this kind of seeing. We begin to recognize the difference between what is transient and what is enduring. We learn to appreciate the beauty of the world without demanding that it provide what only the deeper life of the spirit can offer. We enjoy the gift without mistaking it for the Source.

In this light, becoming "superior to the world" is not about rejecting creation or looking down upon others. It is about freedom. When we no longer depend on the world's shifting conditions for our sense of worth, identity, or peace, we are no longer ruled by them. We can participate fully in life without becoming enslaved by its passing forms.

Perhaps Jesus is inviting us to see through appearances into a deeper reality. The world remains beautiful, but it is not ultimate. Every changing thing points beyond itself. Every temporary joy hints at a greater joy. Every fleeting moment becomes a doorway into the eternal presence that sustains all things.

Today, notice what you may be asking the world to give you. Security, approval, meaning, or completion. Then gently release that burden. Let the world be what it is: a beautiful, passing gift. And let your heart rest in the deeper life that neither fades nor dies.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Beyond what passes*
>
> Exhale: *I rest in what remains*
