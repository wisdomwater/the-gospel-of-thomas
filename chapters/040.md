## 40 - Rooted in What Is Real

> *Jesus said, "A grapevine has been planted outside of the father, but being unsound, it will be pulled up by its roots and destroyed."*

This saying presents a simple image from nature. A vine has been planted, and for a time it may even appear healthy. It may produce leaves, stretch toward the sun, and seem firmly established. Yet something is wrong at its foundation. Its roots do not reach into the source of life. Because of this, its future is already uncertain.

There is a gentle warning here for all of us. Much of what we build in life can appear successful on the surface while lacking deep roots. We may construct an identity based on achievement, approval, wealth, status, or certainty. These things can provide temporary stability, but if they become the foundation of our lives, they eventually reveal their fragility.

The spiritual journey repeatedly invites us beneath appearances. It asks not merely, "What are you building?" but "What are you building upon?" A tree survives storms because of what lies unseen beneath the ground. In the same way, the health of the soul depends less on outward appearance and more on the depth of its connection to what is true, good, and enduring.

Many of us discover this through seasons of disruption. Something we depended upon falls away. A role changes. A relationship shifts. A long-held assumption no longer holds. These moments can feel painful, yet they often reveal where our roots have been placed. What seemed permanent is shown to be temporary. What seemed essential is revealed to be secondary.

The good news is that uprooting is not always a loss. Sometimes what is unsound must be released so that something deeper can grow. Life has a way of clearing away what cannot ultimately sustain us. Though the process may feel unsettling, it creates space for a more authentic rootedness to emerge.

To be rooted in the Father, contemplatively understood, is to draw nourishment from the source of life itself. It is to ground ourselves in divine love rather than fear, in presence rather than distraction, in truth rather than illusion. Such roots may not always be visible, but they provide a strength that endures through changing seasons.

Today, take a moment to consider the roots of your own life. What truly nourishes you? What gives you stability when circumstances shift? What remains when the surface layers are stripped away? Follow those questions gently downward. Beneath them you may discover a deeper source, quietly sustaining you and inviting you to grow.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Root me in Your love*
>
> Exhale: *Ground me in what endures*
