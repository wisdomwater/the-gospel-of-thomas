## 99 - The Family of the Heart

> *The disciples said to him, "Your brothers and your mother are standing outside." He said to them, "Those here who do the will of my father are my brothers and my mother. It is they who will enter the kingdom of my father."*

Someone informs Jesus that his family is waiting outside. It is a simple observation, yet Jesus responds by expanding the meaning of family beyond blood, ancestry, and social ties. He points toward a deeper kind of belonging, one rooted not in biology but in shared participation in the life of God.

Most of us begin life understanding family as those to whom we are related. These relationships can be beautiful gifts, offering love, support, and identity. Yet human experience also teaches us that kinship can arise in unexpected places. We encounter people who understand us deeply, encourage our growth, and walk beside us in ways that feel profoundly familiar. Sometimes the heart recognizes family before the family tree does.

Jesus speaks of those who do the will of the Father. Contemplatively understood, this is not merely obedience to external rules. It is the willingness to align one's life with the movement of Divine Love. It is the choice to participate in compassion, mercy, forgiveness, and truth. Wherever people are living from this deeper center, a spiritual kinship begins to emerge.

The Kingdom is often described as a reality of union rather than separation. In that light, this saying invites us to see one another differently. Beneath our various identities, backgrounds, beliefs, and stories is a shared longing for wholeness and love. The more we awaken to that deeper reality, the more we discover that we belong to one another.

This does not diminish the importance of our earthly families. Rather, it enlarges our understanding of relationship. The circle grows wider. The boundaries become more permeable. The heart learns to recognize brothers, sisters, mothers, and fathers wherever the life of God is being expressed.

Perhaps the invitation today is to consider where this deeper family is already present in your life. Who has helped you become more loving, more awake, more authentic? Who has reflected the presence of God to you through their kindness or wisdom? Give thanks for them. They are signs of the Kingdom.

And perhaps you, too, are called to become such a presence for others. The family of the Kingdom is not built through lineage but through love. It grows whenever people choose to live from the heart of God.
