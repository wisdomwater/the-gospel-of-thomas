## 62 - The Quiet Work of the Soul

> *Jesus said, "It is to those who are worthy of my mysteries that I tell my mysteries. Do not let your left (hand) know what your right (hand) is doing."*

There is something about the deepest truths that resists being grasped through force. Mysteries cannot be conquered. They can only be received. Like a sunrise, they reveal themselves gradually to those who are willing to be present, attentive, and open.

When Jesus speaks of "those who are worthy of my mysteries," perhaps he is not speaking about spiritual superiority or special status. Rather, he may be describing a posture of readiness. A mystery can only be received by a heart that has become quiet enough to notice it. The worthy are not those who know the most, but those who have become receptive.

The second part of the saying offers a surprising clue. *"Do not let your left hand know what your right hand is doing."* On the surface, it speaks of hidden action. Yet it may also point toward an inner simplicity—a way of living that is free from self-consciousness and spiritual performance.

How often do we turn even good things into opportunities for the ego? We notice ourselves being kind and immediately begin constructing a story about our kindness. We make progress on the spiritual path and then become proud of our progress. We seek humility and then become pleased with our humility. The mind is endlessly tempted to turn every gift into an identity.

Jesus invites another way. Let goodness flow naturally. Let prayer deepen quietly. Let love be offered without keeping score. The most profound transformations often happen beneath the surface, beyond the need for recognition—even recognition from ourselves.

The Kingdom grows much like a seed beneath the soil. It does not announce itself. It does not demand attention. It simply grows. In the same way, the soul is transformed through countless small moments of surrender, compassion, silence, and awareness. Much of this sacred work happens in hidden places.

Today, allow yourself to rest in the mystery. You do not need to understand everything. You do not need to measure your progress. Simply be present. Let your acts of love be simple. Let your prayers be quiet. Let your heart remain open. Some of the deepest movements of God occur where no audience is watching—not even the one within yourself.
