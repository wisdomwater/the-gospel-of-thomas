## 101 - The Deeper Birth

> *Jesus said, "Whoever does not hate his father and his mother as I do cannot become a disciple to me. And whoever does not love his father and his mother as I do cannot become a disciple to me. For my mother [...], but my true mother gave me life."*

This saying contains a tension that invites careful listening. Jesus speaks first of "hating" father and mother, then immediately of loving them. Rather than resolving the paradox, he deepens it. As with many of his sayings, the words seem designed to move us beyond a surface reading and into a deeper awareness.

Every one of us is shaped by the family into which we are born. We inherit beliefs, fears, expectations, loyalties, and ways of seeing the world. Much of this inheritance is a gift. Much of it helps us become who we are. Yet part of the spiritual journey involves learning that our deepest identity cannot be confined to what we have inherited.

In this light, "hating" may be understood not as hostility, but as a willingness to loosen our attachment to everything that prevents us from living from our deepest center. There comes a point when we must gently release the demand to be defined by the expectations of others, even those we love. We must be willing to follow truth wherever it leads.

Yet Jesus also speaks of loving father and mother. The goal is not rejection but transformation. When we are no longer bound by unconscious attachment, fear, or obligation, we become capable of a deeper love. We can love others not because they define us, but because we are free. Love becomes less possessive and more spacious. It flows from the soul rather than from dependency.

The saying concludes by pointing toward a "true mother" who gave life. This image suggests a deeper source of identity beyond biological origins. Beneath our earthly story lies a spiritual origin. Beneath the family that raised us is the Divine Life that sustains us. We are born into a household of humanity, but we are also born from the mystery of God.

Perhaps the invitation today is to honor both dimensions. Give thanks for those who shaped your life, while remembering that your deepest self is not limited to their hopes, fears, or expectations. There is a life within you that comes from a deeper source. There is a love within you that is older than every role and wider than every label.

As you awaken to that deeper birth, you may find yourself loving others more fully—not because you belong to them, but because you belong first to God.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Awaken me to my true source*
>
> Exhale: *Teach me to love in freedom*
