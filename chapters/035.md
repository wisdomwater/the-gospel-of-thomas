## 35 - The Strong Man's House

> *Jesus said, "It is not possible for anyone to enter the house of a strong man and take it by force unless he binds his hands; then he will (be able to) ransack his house."*

This saying presents a vivid image: a strong man guarding his house and possessions. No one can simply walk in and take what belongs to him. Something must first happen. The strong man's grip must be loosened before the house can be entered and its contents revealed.

On the surface, the image seems to speak of power and conflict. Yet viewed contemplatively, it may point toward the inner landscape of the soul. Each of us carries deeply rooted habits, fears, attachments, and patterns of thought. These structures often govern our lives without our awareness. They are strong not because they are evil, but because they have been reinforced over many years.

We sometimes imagine that transformation will happen quickly. We decide to change, make a resolution, or adopt a new practice and expect immediate results. Yet the old patterns remain surprisingly resilient. The strong man still occupies the house. Lasting change requires more than good intentions; it requires awareness of what truly holds power within us.

The spiritual journey often begins with learning to recognize these inner forces. Perhaps it is fear that continually shapes our choices. Perhaps it is pride, resentment, insecurity, or the need for control. Whatever the pattern may be, it cannot be transformed while it remains hidden. Awareness is the first loosening of its grip.

Once we see clearly, something remarkable becomes possible. The treasures hidden within the house can be reclaimed. Beneath our conditioned patterns lie gifts waiting to emerge—compassion beneath defensiveness, courage beneath fear, freedom beneath attachment, love beneath self-protection. The goal is not to destroy ourselves, but to liberate what has been covered over.

There is also a gentle reminder here about patience. The strong man is not overcome through force alone. The work of transformation unfolds gradually. Through prayer, reflection, honesty, and grace, old patterns lose their hold. What once seemed immovable begins to soften. The house becomes more spacious, more peaceful, and more aligned with the life of the spirit.

Today, consider what occupies the strong places within you. What habit, fear, or attachment most often takes charge of your inner house? Do not judge it. Simply notice it. Awareness itself is a sacred beginning. As the grip loosens, space opens for something deeper to emerge—a freedom that has been waiting within you all along.
