## 75 - Entering the Inner Chamber

> *Jesus said, "Many are standing at the door, but it is the solitary who will enter the bridal chamber."*

There is a difference between standing at a door and walking through it. Many may gather near the entrance. Many may admire it, discuss it, or wonder what lies beyond. Yet entering requires a step that no one else can take for us.

The image of the bridal chamber carries a sense of intimacy, union, and deep belonging. It speaks of a meeting place where separation gives way to communion. In contemplative terms, it can be seen as the hidden center of the soul—the place where we encounter the Divine not as a distant idea, but as a living presence.

Jesus says it is the solitary who enters. Solitude here need not mean isolation from other people. Rather, it points to a certain inner simplicity. There comes a moment on every spiritual journey when the noise of comparison, approval, expectation, and performance must be set aside. No crowd can accompany us into the deepest places of the heart.

Much of life encourages us to define ourselves through the eyes of others. We learn to wear roles, defend identities, and seek affirmation. Yet beneath all of these layers is a self that exists before any title, accomplishment, or opinion. Solitude is the practice of gently returning to that deeper center. It is the willingness to be alone with what is true.

Paradoxically, this kind of solitude does not make us less connected. It makes genuine connection possible. When we no longer depend on others to tell us who we are, we become freer to love them. When we have rested in the presence of God within, we no longer need to grasp for belonging from the outside. We discover that we are already held.

Perhaps the invitation of this saying is not to withdraw from the world, but to become inwardly quiet enough to enter the sacred room that has always been waiting. The door is near. The threshold is close. Beyond it is not loneliness, but union. Not absence, but presence. Not striving, but rest.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Lead me into stillness*
>
> Exhale: *I rest in Your presence*
