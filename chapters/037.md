## 37 - Without Shame

> *His disciples said, "When will you become revealed to us and when shall we see you?" Jesus said, "When you disrobe without being ashamed and take up your garments and place them under your feet like little children and tread on them, then will you see the son of the living one, and you will not be afraid."*

The disciples ask a question many seekers have asked throughout the ages: "When will we see?" They long for revelation, for clarity, for a direct encounter with the sacred. Jesus responds, not with a date or a sign, but with an image of disrobing, innocence, and freedom from fear.

The garments in this saying may symbolize much more than clothing. Throughout our lives, we accumulate layers of identity. We wear the garments of reputation, success, failure, status, beliefs, roles, and self-images. Some of these garments serve a purpose, but over time we can begin to mistake them for our true selves. We forget who we are beneath them.

Jesus speaks of removing these garments without shame. This is an important detail. Many people hide behind masks because they fear what will happen if they are truly seen. Shame whispers that our imperfections make us unworthy of love or belonging. Yet the spiritual path invites us into a different experience—the discovery that we are already held in grace, even when nothing is hidden.

The image of little children appears again here. Children do not yet carry the same burden of self-consciousness. They are not constantly managing an image or protecting a carefully crafted identity. There is a natural openness and simplicity in them. Jesus points toward this quality of heart, not as immaturity, but as freedom.

To place the garments beneath one's feet is a powerful symbol. It suggests no longer being ruled by the labels and identities that once defined us. We do not necessarily discard our roles in life, but we stop allowing them to determine our deepest worth. We stand upon them rather than beneath them. They become tools rather than masters.

Then comes the promise: "you will see ... and you will not be afraid." Fear often arises from the belief that we must protect something fragile. We fear losing our image, our status, our certainty, or our sense of control. But when we discover the deeper self beneath these coverings, something changes. We realize there is a part of us that does not depend upon any of these things. In that realization, fear begins to loosen its grip.

Today, consider the garments you are wearing. What identities, expectations, or stories have become so familiar that you mistake them for yourself? Imagine setting them down for a moment. Beneath them is the person God has always known—the person who needs no disguise. There, in that place of simplicity and honesty, the sacred becomes easier to see, and the heart becomes less afraid.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Help me release my masks*
>
> Exhale: *And rest without fear*
