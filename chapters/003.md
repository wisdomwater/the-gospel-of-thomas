## 3 - The Kingdom Nearer Than Near

> *Jesus said, "If those who lead you say to you, 'See, the kingdom is in the sky,' then the birds of the sky will precede you. If they say to you, 'It is in the sea,' then the fish will precede you. Rather, the kingdom is inside of you, and it is outside of you. When you come to know yourselves, then you will become known, and you will realize that it is you who are the sons of the living father. But if you will not know yourselves, you dwell in poverty and it is you who are that poverty."*

We often imagine that what we seek is somewhere else. Just beyond the horizon. Hidden in a distant place, a future moment, or a spiritual achievement we have not yet attained. The mind is constantly tempted to search far away for what may already be quietly present.

Jesus gently challenges that impulse. The kingdom is not locked in the sky or buried in the depths of the sea. It is not reserved for those who can travel the farthest or climb the highest. Instead, it is astonishingly near—within you and around you. Closer than your next breath. Present in the ordinary moment you are living right now.

Yet this nearness can be difficult to recognize. We become distracted by noise, driven by fear, or caught in the endless task of becoming someone else. We look for fulfillment outside ourselves while remaining strangers to our own hearts. The spiritual journey is often less about acquiring something new and more about uncovering what has been there all along.

Jesus links this discovery to self-knowledge. To know yourself is not merely to catalog your personality traits or preferences. It is to look honestly and compassionately within. It is to become aware of your fears, your desires, your wounds, your gifts, and the deeper life beneath them all. In that honest seeing, something sacred begins to emerge.

As we come to know ourselves more deeply, we also discover that we are already known. Beneath every mask and role is a life held within Divine Love. The saying points toward a profound dignity hidden within every person. We are not accidents wandering through a meaningless world. We belong. We arise from a deeper source than we often realize.

The poverty Jesus speaks of is not a lack of possessions but a lack of awareness. It is the experience of living disconnected from our own deepest reality. Yet the invitation remains open. Turn inward with honesty. Look outward with wonder. The kingdom is not somewhere else. It is woven through your being and shining through the world around you, waiting to be recognized.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *The kingdom is here*
>
