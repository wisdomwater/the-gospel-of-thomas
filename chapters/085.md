## 85 - Called Beyond Adam

> *Jesus said, "Adam came into being from a great power and a great wealth, but he did not become worthy of you. For had he been worthy, he would not have experienced death."*

This saying speaks of Adam, the ancient symbol of humanity, and yet its focus seems to rest not on Adam himself but on those listening. Jesus begins with humanity's noble origin. Adam came from "a great power and a great wealth." The source of human life is not poverty but abundance. We emerge from mystery, gifted with breath, awareness, and the capacity to know and love.

Yet the saying quickly turns. Adam, despite this glorious beginning, did not attain what was possible for him. He experienced death. The words are mysterious, but they invite us to consider the difference between merely possessing life and fully awakening to it.

Many people move through life identified only with what is temporary. We know ourselves as bodies that age, personalities that change, and stories that unfold across time. These things are real, yet they are not the whole of who we are. When we live exclusively from this surface identity, we remain bound to the cycles of gain and loss, success and failure, birth and death.

The contemplative tradition often speaks of another dimension of life hidden within the ordinary self. Beneath the changing personality is a deeper center rooted in God. This deeper life is not created by achievement, nor can it be diminished by circumstance. It is the life of the Spirit already present within us, waiting to be recognized.

Perhaps this is why Jesus says Adam was not worthy of "you." The saying can be heard as a reminder that something greater has been placed before humanity. We are not merely creatures struggling for survival. We are bearers of divine possibility. We are invited into a consciousness that transcends fear and a life that participates in something eternal.

The invitation today is not to reject your humanity but to look more deeply into it. The same power and wealth that brought Adam into being are present within you now. Beneath your worries, your limitations, and your changing circumstances lives a deeper life that cannot be reduced to mortality alone. As you awaken to that life, you may discover that what is most true about you has never been touched by death.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Awaken me to deeper life*
>
> Exhale: *I rest in what endures*
