## 95 - Giving Without Keeping Score

> *Jesus said, "If you have money, do not lend it at interest, but give it to one from whom you will not get it back."*

At first glance, this saying appears to speak about money. Yet beneath the practical image lies a deeper invitation into the nature of generosity. Jesus describes a kind of giving that is free from calculation, a gift offered without the expectation of return.

Much of human life operates through exchange. We give in order to receive. We invest in hopes of gain. Even in relationships, we can find ourselves quietly keeping score—measuring affection, attention, gratitude, or recognition. There is nothing unusual about this. It is one of the ways the ego seeks security and fairness.

Yet Jesus points toward another way of living. What if generosity did not depend upon repayment? What if the value of a gift was not determined by what it brought back to us? Such giving is rare because it asks us to release control over the outcome. It invites us to trust abundance rather than scarcity.

This saying is not merely about financial resources. We can lend our time expecting appreciation. We can offer kindness hoping for acknowledgment. We can share wisdom wanting admiration. Whenever we give with hidden expectations, our gift becomes entangled with a transaction. The heart remains attached to the return.

The contemplative path gently teaches a different rhythm. Divine Love pours itself out continuously without demanding repayment. The sun shines on fields that never thank it. Rain falls on gardens that cannot return the favor. Love, in its purest form, gives because giving is its nature.

Of course, wisdom and healthy boundaries still matter. This saying is not an invitation to neglect discernment or enable harm. Rather, it calls us to examine the motives beneath our generosity. Are we giving to gain something, or are we giving because love has already made us rich?

Perhaps the invitation today is to offer something freely—a kind word, a listening ear, a moment of patience, an act of generosity—with no need to track the result. In doing so, you may discover a surprising freedom. The heart becomes lighter when it no longer carries a ledger. The gift has already fulfilled its purpose by being given.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Teach me generous love*
>
> Exhale: *I release the need for return*
