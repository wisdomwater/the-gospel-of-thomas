## 72 - Beyond Dividing

> *A man said to him, "Tell my brothers to divide my father's possessions with me." He said to him, "O man, who has made me a divider?" He turned to his disciples and said to them, "I am not a divider, am I?"*

A simple request is brought to Jesus. It is a familiar human concern: fairness, inheritance, the distribution of what belongs to whom. Few things create tension more quickly than questions of ownership and entitlement. We want clear lines, clear answers, and someone to declare what is ours.

Yet Jesus responds in an unexpected way. He does not take sides. He does not step into the role that has been offered to him. Instead, he asks a question that gently shifts the entire conversation. "Who has made me a divider?" His attention seems to move beneath the dispute itself.

Much of life is spent drawing boundaries. We divide possessions, opinions, groups, identities, successes, and failures. The mind is constantly sorting reality into categories: mine and yours, us and them, gain and loss. Some distinctions are necessary for daily life, yet they can also become habits that separate us from one another and from the deeper unity that underlies all things.

Perhaps Jesus is inviting his listeners to notice that the spiritual path is not primarily about dividing but about seeing. Before deciding what belongs to whom, there is the more fundamental question of who we are. Beneath our claims, fears, and grievances lives a deeper self that does not depend upon what it possesses. That self is already held within the abundance of God.

This does not mean practical matters are unimportant. Relationships, responsibilities, and justice still matter. But the heart can approach them differently. When we are no longer defined by what we hold, we become freer to act with wisdom rather than anxiety, with generosity rather than fear.

The invitation of this saying may be to notice where the mind is dividing and to gently return to what unites. Beneath every label and every claim is a shared humanity. Beneath every conflict is a deeper Presence. The Kingdom is not built by drawing harder lines. It is discovered when we begin to see beyond them.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Beyond what divides*
>
> Exhale: *I rest in what unites*
