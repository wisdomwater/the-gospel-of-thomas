## 49 - Returning to What You Already Are

> *Jesus said, "Blessed are the solitary and elect, for you will find the kingdom. For you are from it, and to it you will return."*

There is a kind of solitude that is not loneliness. It is the quiet place within us that exists beneath the noise of expectations, roles, and distractions. It is the place we encounter when we stop performing, stop comparing, and simply become present to our own lives. Perhaps this is the solitude Jesus speaks of here.

Many people fear being alone because silence can reveal what has long been hidden. Yet the spiritual journey often includes moments of stepping back from the crowd—not necessarily physically, but inwardly. We begin to listen less to the competing voices around us and more to the deeper wisdom that quietly waits within.

Jesus calls such people blessed. Not because they have separated themselves from others, but because they have discovered a center that cannot be shaken by the changing opinions of the world. They have learned to dwell in a deeper place than approval or rejection, success or failure. They have become acquainted with the ground of their being.

Then comes the beautiful promise: *"You will find the kingdom."* Not because the Kingdom is far away, but because stillness allows us to notice what has always been present. We spend so much of life searching for what cannot be lost. The Kingdom is not merely a destination waiting somewhere ahead. It is the reality from which we have come and in which we already live and move.

*"For you are from it, and to it you will return."* These words carry a profound sense of belonging. We are not strangers wandering through an indifferent universe. We arise from a deeper Source. The life that animates us, sustains us, and calls to us is the very life to which we belong. The journey home is not a journey toward something foreign. It is a return to what is most true.

Perhaps the invitation today is simply to rest. Stop striving for a moment. Stop trying to become worthy of what has already been freely given. Sit quietly. Breathe deeply. Let yourself remember that beneath all the changing circumstances of life, you belong to the Kingdom, and the Kingdom belongs to you.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *I come from Love*
>
> Exhale: *I return to Love*
