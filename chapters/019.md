## 19 - The Life Beneath the Life

> *Jesus said, "Blessed is he who came into being before he came into being. If you become my disciples and listen to my words, these stones will minister to you. For there are five trees for you in Paradise which remain undisturbed summer and winter and whose leaves do not fall. Whoever becomes acquainted with them will not experience death."*

This saying unfolds like a dream filled with sacred symbols. A person who existed before coming into being. Stones that minister. Trees whose leaves never fall. Rather than inviting us to solve a puzzle, the saying invites us to enter a deeper way of seeing. It points beyond appearances toward a reality that is always present, yet often overlooked.

What does it mean to have "come into being before coming into being"? Perhaps it points toward the truth that our deepest identity is more than the personality we have constructed over time. Before our achievements and failures, before our fears and ambitions, before all the stories we tell about ourselves, there is something more fundamental. There is a sacred life hidden within us, rooted in the very life of God.

Many spiritual traditions speak of this deeper self—not as something we create, but something we awaken to. The journey is not about becoming someone entirely new. It is about remembering what has always been true. We spend years building an identity, only to discover that the most precious part of us was present long before we knew how to name it.

Jesus then says that even the stones will minister to those who truly listen. This beautiful image suggests a transformed relationship with the world itself. When we become attentive, life begins speaking from every direction. A sunrise, a tree, a quiet conversation, even an ordinary stone can become a teacher. The sacred is no longer confined to special places. It shines through all things.

The image of the evergreen trees deepens this invitation. While seasons change and leaves fall elsewhere, these trees remain undisturbed. They symbolize a life that is not dependent on circumstances. Beneath the changing weather of our emotions and experiences is a deeper rootedness, an inner life that remains green through every season. To become acquainted with this life is to discover something death cannot touch.

Today, take a moment to rest beneath those symbolic trees. Let yourself become still enough to hear what the stones might say. Beneath the changing story of your life is a deeper story, written in love before you ever learned your own name. The more you become acquainted with that hidden life, the more you discover that what is most real within you has always belonged to eternity.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Awaken me to my true self*
>
> Exhale: *Root me in eternal life*
