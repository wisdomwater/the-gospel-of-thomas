## 6 - The Simplicity of an Honest Heart

> *His disciples questioned him and said to him, "Do you want us to fast? How shall we pray? Shall we give alms? What diet shall we observe?" Jesus said, "Do not tell lies, and do not do what you hate, for all things are plain in the sight of heaven. For nothing hidden will not become manifest, and nothing covered will remain without being uncovered."*

The disciples ask the kinds of questions many sincere seekers ask. What practices should we follow? What disciplines should we observe? What is the correct way to live? These are natural questions, born from a genuine desire to walk a spiritual path with integrity.

Yet Jesus responds in a surprising way. Rather than offering a list of rituals or rules, he directs attention toward honesty and inner alignment. His answer shifts the focus from outward observance to inward truthfulness. Before worrying about spiritual techniques, begin with sincerity.

There is a subtle temptation in every spiritual journey to become preoccupied with appearances. We can become concerned with looking wise, sounding spiritual, or following the right practices while neglecting what is happening within. But the heart cannot be transformed through performance. True growth begins when we are willing to be honest about who we are and how we are living.

"Do not tell lies" can be heard not only as a command about our words but as an invitation to stop deceiving ourselves. We often create stories that protect us from uncomfortable truths. We hide our fears behind confidence, our wounds behind busyness, or our longing behind distraction. Yet healing begins when we gently allow the truth to come into the light.

Likewise, "do not do what you hate" invites a deeper integrity. When our actions continually violate our conscience, our hearts become divided. Peace grows when our inner values and outward actions begin to move together. We become more whole, more centered, and more able to live from the deepest part of ourselves.

Jesus concludes by reminding us that everything hidden eventually comes into view. This need not be heard as a threat. It can also be heard as a promise. The truth wants to emerge. The light is always working its way into forgotten corners. What is buried can be healed. What is concealed can be integrated. The spiritual life is not about becoming someone else. It is about allowing what is true to be revealed.
