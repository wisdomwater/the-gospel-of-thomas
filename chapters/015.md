## 15 - The Source Beyond All Forms

> *Jesus said, "When you see one who was not born of woman, prostrate yourselves on your faces and worship him. That one is your father."*

This saying arrives wrapped in mystery. Who is this one "not born of woman"? The image seems impossible, pointing beyond ordinary categories and beyond the familiar world of beginnings and endings. Rather than offering an easy answer, the saying invites us to look beyond appearances toward a deeper source from which all life flows.

Most of our attention is occupied by forms. We notice names, bodies, roles, histories, and identities. We learn to define ourselves by what can be seen and measured. Yet beneath every form lies a mystery that cannot be captured by any description. There is a life within life, a presence beneath all appearances, quietly sustaining everything.

The invitation to bow down may not be about fear or submission so much as reverence. There are moments when the soul encounters something vast enough to silence its endless explanations. In those moments, the mind stops grasping and the heart simply recognizes. Wonder takes the place of certainty. Awe becomes its own kind of prayer.

Perhaps the saying points toward the Divine Source itself—the One who is not produced by the world but from whom the world continually arises. We spend much of our lives searching among created things for what can only be found in the Creator. We look for lasting fulfillment in passing forms. Yet every beautiful thing eventually becomes a signpost pointing beyond itself.

There is also a quieter way this saying may speak to us. Within each person exists a depth that was never created by the ego's stories. Beneath our fears, achievements, failures, and labels is a sacred center that remains connected to the Source. To discover that center is to begin remembering who we truly are and where we truly belong.

Today, allow yourself to pause before the mystery. You do not need to solve it. Simply let yourself be drawn into reverence for the Life that exists before every name and beyond every form. The One you seek may be closer than your next breath, quietly present beneath all things.
