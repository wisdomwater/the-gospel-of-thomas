## 103 - Watching the Threshold

> *Jesus said, "Fortunate is the man who knows where the brigands will enter, so that he may get up, muster his domain, and arm himself before they invade."*

There are moments in life when we can sense a disturbance long before it fully arrives. A restless thought, a familiar frustration, a quiet anxiety—at first they seem small and insignificant. Yet if left unnoticed, they can gradually pull us away from our deeper center.

The saying invites us into the gift of awareness. It suggests that wisdom is not merely reacting to what happens after it appears, but learning to recognize the places where our peace is most easily unsettled. Such awareness is not a cause for fear. It is an invitation to wakefulness.

Most of us carry patterns that revisit us throughout our lives. Certain circumstances awaken old wounds. Certain voices stir self-doubt. Certain disappointments tempt us toward discouragement. Spiritual growth often begins not by eliminating these experiences but by recognizing them sooner and meeting them with compassion and clarity.

When we become attentive to our inner landscape, we gain the freedom to choose our response. We are no longer carried away by every passing emotion or impulse. We learn to pause, to breathe, and to return to the quiet presence that has always been waiting beneath the noise.

This kind of watchfulness is not tense or defensive. It is gentle and steady. It arises from a desire to care for what is sacred within us. Like a gardener tending a beloved garden, we learn to notice what nourishes life and what quietly drains it.

Perhaps the blessing in this saying is not found in controlling every circumstance, but in becoming more awake. The more clearly we see ourselves, the more readily we can return to love, presence, and peace. Awareness becomes a doorway through which grace continually enters.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Awaken my eyes*
>
> Exhale: *Keep me present*
