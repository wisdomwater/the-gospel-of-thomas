## 90 - The Rest Beneath the Burden

> *Jesus said, "Come unto me, for my yoke is easy and my lordship is mild, and you will find repose for yourselves."*

These words arrive like a gentle invitation at the end of a long journey. There is no command here, no demand to strive harder or prove oneself worthy. Instead, there is a simple call: *Come.* The spiritual life begins and continues with this movement of turning toward the One who calls us.

Many of us carry burdens we were never meant to bear alone. Some are visible—responsibilities, losses, disappointments, and daily concerns. Others are hidden—self-judgment, anxiety, regret, and the quiet pressure to become more than we believe we are. Over time, these burdens can become so familiar that we forget how heavy they have become.

Jesus speaks of a yoke, but not one that crushes. A yoke joins rather than isolates. It allows a burden to be shared. In this saying, the image suggests a way of life lived in partnership with Divine Love rather than in lonely striving. We are not asked to carry everything ourselves. We are invited into a relationship that lightens what once felt unbearable.

His lordship is described as mild. This is a remarkable image of spiritual authority. The Divine does not rule through fear, coercion, or domination. The guidance of God is gentle. It does not force the heart open but patiently invites it. Like sunlight entering a room through an open window, grace works through welcome rather than compulsion.

The promise at the end of the saying is repose. Not merely the absence of activity, but a deeper restfulness of soul. This is the peace that emerges when we stop fighting reality, stop trying to secure ourselves through endless effort, and begin trusting the Presence that has accompanied us all along. It is the rest found at the center of being.

Perhaps the invitation today is simply to come. Come with your questions, your hopes, your weariness, and your unfinished journey. Come without needing to have everything figured out. The path of transformation is not another burden to carry. It is an invitation to discover that beneath all striving there is already a place of rest.

The Kingdom, the Light, the hidden treasure, the bridal chamber, the living water—all the images we have encountered along the way ultimately point toward this same reality: a life rooted in Divine Presence. And in that Presence, the soul finally finds its home.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *I come as I am*
>
> Exhale: *I rest in Your peace*
