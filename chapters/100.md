## 100 - What Belongs to God

> *They showed Jesus a gold coin and said to him, "Caesar's men demand taxes from us." He said to them, "Give Caesar what belongs to Caesar, give God what belongs to God, and give me what is mine."*

A coin is placed before Jesus. Like so many objects in the Gospels, it becomes more than it first appears. The question concerns taxes, obligations, and competing claims of loyalty. Yet Jesus responds in a way that redirects attention from the coin itself to a deeper question: *What truly belongs to whom?*

Some things clearly belong to the world of commerce, government, and daily affairs. We all live within systems and responsibilities. We pay bills, keep commitments, and participate in the structures of society. Jesus does not deny these realities. The coin may be given where the coin belongs.

But then he speaks of God. The conversation suddenly moves beyond economics and into the realm of identity. If the coin bears Caesar's image, what bears the image of God? The contemplative heart cannot help but turn inward at this question.

The ancient tradition tells us that humanity is made in the image of God. If that is so, then our deepest self belongs not to the kingdoms of this world but to the Divine Source from which we came. Our possessions may belong to us for a time. Our titles, accomplishments, and roles may have their place. Yet the soul itself is God's.

Much of spiritual life can be understood as learning to return what belongs to God. We offer our attention, our love, our fears, our hopes, our joys, and our wounds. We stop treating our lives as private possessions and begin receiving them as sacred gifts. The more we surrender, the more we discover that we lose nothing essential. We simply return to our true home.

Then there is the mysterious final phrase: *"Give me what is mine."* Perhaps the invitation is personal. The living Christ asks not merely for belief or admiration, but for relationship. Not just a portion of our lives, but our presence. The teacher asks for the heart of the disciple.

Perhaps today the invitation is to pause and consider what claims your loyalty. What are you giving your energy, attention, and devotion to? Some things belong to the passing world and should be held lightly. But your deepest self belongs to God. And when what belongs to God is freely offered back, the soul discovers a freedom no earthly kingdom can provide.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *I belong to You*
>
> Exhale: *I return my heart in trust*
