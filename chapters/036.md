## 36 - Beyond the Garments

> *Jesus said, "Do not be concerned from morning until evening and from evening until morning about what you will wear."*

At first glance, this saying seems to address a simple concern: clothing. Yet Jesus points toward something much deeper than garments. He speaks to the tendency of the human mind to become preoccupied with appearances, security, and the endless worries that occupy our thoughts from one day to the next.

Most of us know what it is like to carry concerns from morning until evening and from evening until morning. One worry gives way to another. We think about how we are perceived, whether we have enough, whether we are doing enough, whether we measure up. The mind becomes caught in an unending cycle of planning, comparing, and protecting.

Clothing can be understood symbolically as well. Throughout life, we wear many garments that are not made of cloth. We clothe ourselves in roles, identities, achievements, opinions, and carefully constructed images. We become attached to these coverings and fear what might happen if they were taken away. Yet beneath every garment is the simple reality of who we are.

Jesus gently invites us to loosen our grip on these concerns. The kingdom is not found through endless self-management. It is not discovered by perfecting our image or securing every detail of the future. It is found when we become present enough to encounter the life already unfolding within and around us.

This does not mean neglecting practical responsibilities. Rather, it means refusing to let anxiety become the ruler of our hearts. There is a difference between caring for what is needed and living in constant worry about what might happen. One arises from wisdom; the other arises from fear.

The deeper invitation of this saying is trust. Trust that you are more than the roles you wear. Trust that your value does not depend on appearances. Trust that the sacred presence sustaining the universe is also present in your own life. The soul begins to breathe more freely when it no longer carries the burden of proving its worth.

Today, notice what concerns have been circling through your mind. Notice the garments you feel compelled to wear for the approval of others or for your own sense of security. Then, if only for a moment, set them down. Beneath them is something simple, beautiful, and already whole. Rest there awhile.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Free me from anxious striving*
>
> Exhale: *Teach me to trust*
