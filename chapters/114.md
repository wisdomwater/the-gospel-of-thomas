## 114 - Beyond Division

> *Simon Peter said to him, "Let Mary leave us, for women are not worthy of life." Jesus said, "I myself shall lead her in order to make her male, so that she too may become a living spirit resembling you males. For every woman who will make herself male will enter the kingdom of heaven."*

This saying begins with exclusion. One voice seeks to draw a boundary around who belongs and who does not. Peter speaks from a world of divisions, categories, and judgments. He sees separation where the kingdom sees possibility.

Jesus responds in a way that sounds strange to modern ears, yet throughout this gospel he repeatedly points beyond opposites. Again and again, he speaks of making the two one, of reconciling what appears divided, of discovering a deeper unity beneath outward distinctions. The movement is always toward wholeness.

Every human being carries inner divisions. We divide strength from tenderness, action from contemplation, courage from compassion, certainty from wonder. We often identify with one side while rejecting the other. Yet what we reject does not disappear. It waits patiently in the shadows, longing to be welcomed home.

The spiritual journey is not about becoming someone else. It is about becoming whole. The language of this saying may point toward a transformation in which old divisions lose their power. The labels and categories that once defined us become less important than the living spirit that animates us all.

Seen in this light, Mary's inclusion becomes the heart of the story. The one whom others would exclude is invited forward. The one considered unworthy is revealed to belong. Divine Love does not affirm the boundary; it dissolves it. The kingdom is not enlarged by pushing people away, but by drawing them into a deeper unity.

Perhaps this is the final invitation of the Gospel of Thomas. Beyond all the opposites that shape our lives lies a deeper reality. Beyond insider and outsider, worthy and unworthy, male and female, there is the living spirit. To enter the kingdom is to awaken to that deeper identity and to recognize it in others as well.

Today, notice where division still lives within you. Is there a part of yourself you have rejected? Is there a person you find difficult to embrace? Bring both gently into the light of awareness. The path of wholeness is not found through exclusion but through integration, where what was separated is brought together in love.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Make us one*
>
> Exhale: *Beyond every division*
