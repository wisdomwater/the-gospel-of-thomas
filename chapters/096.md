## 96 - The Hidden Leaven

> *Jesus said, "The kingdom of the father is like a certain woman. She took a little leaven, concealed it in some dough, and made it into large loaves. Let him who has ears hear."*

The Kingdom arrives in this saying not with trumpets or spectacle, but with a woman quietly baking bread. A small amount of leaven is hidden within the dough. It disappears from sight. Nothing dramatic seems to happen. Yet over time, the entire batch is transformed.

This is often how the spiritual life unfolds. We may expect transformation to come through grand experiences, sudden revelations, or extraordinary moments. Yet much of God's work happens quietly, beneath the surface of awareness. A single insight, a small act of forgiveness, a brief moment of prayer, or a gentle shift in perspective can begin a process whose full effects are not immediately visible.

The woman conceals the leaven. This detail is important. The most powerful changes are not always the most obvious. Seeds grow underground long before they break through the soil. Healing often begins before we can measure it. Love does much of its work in hidden places within the heart.

There are seasons when we become discouraged because we cannot see progress. We look at ourselves and notice the same struggles, the same habits, the same questions. Yet transformation rarely moves in a straight line. The leaven continues its work even when the dough appears unchanged. Something unseen is unfolding.

The Kingdom, according to this image, is not merely a destination waiting in the future. It is an active presence already at work within ordinary life. Every act of kindness, every movement toward awareness, every moment of surrender becomes part of this quiet transformation. The Divine works from the inside out.

Perhaps the invitation today is to trust the hidden process. You may not be able to see all that is changing within you. The prayer offered in silence, the compassion extended to another, the willingness to begin again—these small things matter more than you know. The leaven is at work.

Let those with ears hear this gentle truth: God often transforms lives not through force, but through patient love. And what begins as something small can, in time, become a feast.
