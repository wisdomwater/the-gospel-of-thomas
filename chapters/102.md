## 102 - The Dog in the Manger

> *Jesus said, "Woe to the pharisees, for they are like a dog sleeping in the manger of oxen, for neither does he eat nor does he let the oxen eat."*

Jesus paints a vivid image. A dog lies in a manger filled with food meant for oxen. The dog has no use for the hay. It cannot benefit from it. Yet by occupying the manger, it prevents the oxen from receiving the nourishment intended for them. The tragedy is not merely that the dog remains hungry, but that others are kept from being fed as well.

At its heart, this saying speaks about obstruction. Sometimes we encounter attitudes, habits, or systems that neither bring life nor allow life to flourish. They occupy space without serving their purpose. They cling to control without offering nourishment. What should be a source of blessing becomes a barrier instead.

While Jesus addresses a particular group, the saying invites us to look inward rather than outward. We all have moments when we become the dog in the manger. We cling to old assumptions that no longer nourish us. We hold tightly to opinions, fears, or grievances that prevent new life from emerging. We guard territory we do not truly need while blocking the growth of others and ourselves.

The contemplative path asks us to notice where we are withholding life. Are there places where fear prevents generosity? Are there wounds that have hardened into defensiveness? Are there beliefs we no longer question simply because they feel familiar? Awareness begins when we honestly acknowledge what we are protecting and why.

Yet this saying is not ultimately about blame. It is about freedom. The manger was meant to feed the oxen. The gifts of God are meant to flow. Wisdom is meant to illuminate. Love is meant to be shared. When we release our grip on what we do not need to possess, nourishment becomes available once again.

Perhaps the invitation today is to ask a simple question: *Where am I blocking what longs to flow?* The answer may reveal an opportunity for grace. A small act of openness, humility, or generosity can clear the manger and make room for life to flourish.

The Kingdom grows wherever nourishment is shared freely. And often the first step is simply moving aside so that love can do its work.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Show me where I am holding on*
>
> Exhale: *Help me make room for life*
