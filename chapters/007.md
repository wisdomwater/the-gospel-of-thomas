## 7 - What You Feed Becomes You

> *Jesus said, "Blessed is the lion which becomes man when consumed by man; and cursed is the man whom the lion consumes, and the lion becomes man."*

This saying arrives as a riddle. It presents an image both strange and memorable: a lion and a human, each capable of consuming the other, each capable of being transformed by the encounter. Rather than rushing to solve the puzzle, we might first sit quietly with it. What within us is wild, powerful, instinctive, and untamed? What within us is conscious, aware, and capable of choosing a deeper way?

The lion may symbolize those forces that live within every human heart—anger and desire, fear and ambition, hunger and impulse. These energies are not enemies. They are part of our nature. Yet they can either serve our deeper life or come to dominate it. The question is not whether these forces exist, but what relationship we have with them.

When the lion is "consumed by man," the wild energy is not destroyed but integrated. Strength becomes courage. Desire becomes creativity. Passion becomes compassion. The raw energies of life are gathered into something larger and more conscious. What once threatened to rule us becomes a source of wisdom and vitality.

But when the lion consumes the man, the opposite occurs. We become captive to whatever impulse shouts the loudest. Fear dictates our choices. Anger narrows our vision. Desire becomes compulsion. Instead of living from our deepest center, we are carried along by forces we no longer understand. The lion becomes the master rather than the servant.

The contemplative path is not a rejection of our humanity but a process of integration. We learn to welcome every part of ourselves into awareness. Nothing is denied, and nothing is allowed to take the throne. Through attention, compassion, and presence, the divided self gradually becomes whole.

Perhaps the blessing in this saying is the blessing of becoming fully human. Not by suppressing the powerful energies within us, but by holding them in the light of awareness until they are transformed. As we learn to live from that deeper center, even the lion finds its rightful place within the harmony of the soul.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Gather me together*
>
> Exhale: *Make me whole*
