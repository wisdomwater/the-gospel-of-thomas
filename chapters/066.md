## 66 - The Rejected Stone

> *Jesus said, "Show me the stone which the builders have rejected. That one is the cornerstone."*

There is a beautiful paradox at the heart of this saying. The very stone that was dismissed, overlooked, or cast aside becomes the cornerstone—the stone upon which everything else is built. What seemed insignificant turns out to be essential.

We often make quick judgments about what is valuable. We celebrate what appears strong, successful, impressive, or useful. At the same time, we may overlook what seems weak, ordinary, broken, or unimportant. Yet life has a way of surprising us. Again and again, the deepest treasures emerge from places we least expected.

This pattern appears not only in the world around us but within our own hearts. Many of us carry parts of ourselves that we have rejected. Old wounds. Unanswered questions. Vulnerabilities. Aspects of our personality that do not fit the image we wish to present. We hide them away, assuming they have little value.

Yet the contemplative path often leads us back to these forgotten places. What we reject may contain the very lesson we need. The wound we avoid may become the source of compassion. The weakness we resist may teach us humility. The uncertainty we fear may open us to deeper trust. Sometimes the cornerstone is hidden within the stone we were most eager to discard.

This saying also invites us to look differently at others. The people who are ignored, dismissed, or underestimated often carry wisdom that the powerful overlook. Divine Love has a habit of appearing in unexpected places. The sacred is not always found where the world tells us to look.

Jesus continually points toward this reversal. The last become first. The poor are blessed. Children reveal the Kingdom. The rejected stone becomes the cornerstone. Again and again, we are invited to see with new eyes rather than relying on familiar assumptions.

Today, consider what you may have rejected in yourself or in your life. Is there a part of your story you wish had never happened? A weakness you have tried to hide? A person you have overlooked? Gently bring it into the light. The stone you dismissed may be carrying a gift. What appears insignificant today may become the foundation of tomorrow's wisdom.
