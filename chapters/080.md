## 80 - Living Beyond the Surface

> *Jesus said, "He who has recognized the world has found the body, but he who has found the body is superior to the world."*

This saying arrives as a mystery. Its words seem simple, yet they open into deeper questions. What does it mean to recognize the world? What does it mean to find the body? Rather than rushing toward an answer, we can allow the saying to invite us into a deeper way of seeing.

To recognize the world may be to awaken to the nature of our ordinary experience. We begin to see how much of life is shaped by desires, fears, habits, ambitions, and endless striving. We notice the patterns that pull our attention outward and keep us moving from one pursuit to another. This recognition is an important step. We cannot awaken from a dream until we realize we are dreaming.

Yet Jesus points beyond recognition alone. There is something more to be found. The body, in a contemplative sense, can be understood as our immediate, embodied experience of life itself. While the mind races through memories and expectations, the body remains rooted in the present moment. It is here, in breath and sensation, in presence and awareness, that we encounter reality directly.

Many people live primarily in thoughts about life rather than in life itself. We become caught in stories about the past or worries about the future. The body gently calls us back. Every breath reminds us that we are here. Every heartbeat testifies to a life unfolding beneath our constant mental activity. To "find the body" may be to return to this sacred immediacy.

When we learn to inhabit the present moment, the world loses some of its power to dominate us. We are no longer swept away by every fear, every craving, or every passing opinion. We discover a deeper center within ourselves—a place of awareness that can observe the movements of life without being consumed by them.

Perhaps this is what it means to be "superior to the world." Not that we reject the world or stand above it, but that we are no longer ruled by its restless currents. We become grounded in something deeper. The present moment becomes a doorway to freedom, and the life of God becomes something experienced rather than merely imagined.

Today, let the body be your teacher. Feel your feet upon the ground. Notice your breath. Listen to the quiet aliveness within you. The sacred may be closer than your next thought. It may already be present in the simple miracle of being here.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Awaken me to this moment*
>
> Exhale: *I rest in what is real*
