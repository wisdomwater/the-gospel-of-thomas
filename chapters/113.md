## 113 - Already Here

> *His disciples said to him, "When will the kingdom come?" Jesus said, "It will not come by waiting for it. It will not be a matter of saying 'here it is' or 'there it is.' Rather, the kingdom of the father is spread out upon the earth, and men do not see it."*

The disciples ask a question that human beings have asked for centuries: *When will it come?* We often imagine that fulfillment, peace, enlightenment, or the presence of God lies somewhere in the future. We look ahead, waiting for a day when everything will finally make sense and life will become what we hope it can be.

Jesus responds by gently turning their attention away from the horizon. The kingdom is not something that arrives like a distant traveler. It is not hidden in a particular place, waiting to be discovered by those with the right map. Instead, he says it is already spread out upon the earth.

What a remarkable possibility. The sacred is not absent. The kingdom is not delayed. The problem is not that God has failed to arrive. The problem, Jesus suggests, is that we do not see. We are often so occupied with searching elsewhere that we overlook the reality already surrounding us.

This is a challenge that touches every part of life. We postpone joy until circumstances improve. We postpone gratitude until problems are solved. We postpone peace until the future becomes more certain. Yet life unfolds only in the present moment. The kingdom cannot be encountered tomorrow because tomorrow never arrives. It can only be encountered here.

To see the kingdom requires a different kind of vision. It is the vision that notices beauty in ordinary things. It is the awareness that recognizes love in small acts of kindness. It is the openness that senses the sacred woven through conversations, sunlight, silence, laughter, and even moments of sorrow. What changes is not the world itself but the eyes with which we see it.

The invitation of this saying is wonderfully simple. Stop waiting for life to begin. Stop searching endlessly for what may already be present. Pause. Breathe. Notice. The kingdom may be closer than you imagine—not hidden beyond the stars, but quietly shining through the very moment you are living.

Today, take a few moments to look at your surroundings with fresh eyes. Listen more carefully. Walk a little slower. Become present to what is already here. The kingdom may not need to arrive. It may simply need to be seen.
