## 16 - The Fire That Divides

> *Jesus said, "Men think, perhaps, that it is peace which I have come to cast upon the world. They do not know that it is dissension which I have come to cast upon the earth: fire, sword, and war. For there will be five in a house: three will be against two, and two against three, the father against the son, and the son against the father. And they will stand solitary."*

At first glance, this saying feels unsettling. We often imagine the spiritual life as a path toward harmony, comfort, and agreement. Yet Jesus speaks of fire, division, and standing alone. The words challenge our assumptions and invite us to look more deeply at what true peace actually requires.

There is a kind of peace that comes from avoiding conflict. We learn to keep certain questions buried, to silence parts of ourselves, or to follow expectations that do not truly fit our deepest calling. This peace may feel comfortable for a time, but it is often fragile. Beneath the surface, something remains unresolved, waiting to be seen.

The fire Jesus speaks of may be the fire of awakening. When light enters a dark room, it reveals what was previously hidden. Old beliefs, inherited patterns, fears, and attachments can no longer remain unquestioned. The soul begins to distinguish between what is true and what is merely familiar. This inner sorting can feel like a sword dividing one thing from another.

Sometimes this transformation creates tension not only within us but around us. As we grow, some relationships shift. Expectations that once shaped our lives lose their power. The person we are becoming may no longer fit comfortably into the roles others have assigned us. This does not mean we stop loving others. Rather, we begin to love from a deeper place, one that is no longer governed by fear or the need for approval.

The saying concludes with a striking image: "they will stand solitary." This solitude need not be understood as loneliness. There comes a stage in every spiritual journey when we must stand in the truth we have seen, even when no one else fully understands it. We can receive wisdom from others, but no one can walk the deepest part of the path for us. There is a sacred aloneness in becoming who we truly are.

Yet this solitude is not abandonment. The deeper we travel into our own center, the more we discover that we are held by a Presence greater than ourselves. The fire that separates is also the fire that purifies. What falls away was never meant to define us. What remains is the quiet, steady life of the soul—free, awake, and rooted in love.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Burn away what is false*
>
> Exhale: *Root me in what is true*
