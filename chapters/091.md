## 91 - Reading This Moment

> *They said to him, "Tell us who you are so that we may believe in you." He said to them, "You read the face of the sky and of the earth, but you have not recognized the one who is before you, and you do not know how to read this moment."*

The people ask a familiar question: *Who are you?* They want certainty. They want a clear definition that will settle their doubts and tell them what to believe. It is a deeply human desire. We often feel more comfortable with answers than with mystery.

Yet Jesus responds in an unexpected way. Rather than giving a direct answer, he points to their inability to recognize what is already before them. They can read signs in the sky. They can interpret the seasons of the earth. They can analyze and evaluate the world around them. But they have missed the reality standing in their midst.

How often do we do the same? We become skilled at understanding many things. We learn systems, theories, and explanations. We analyze the past and speculate about the future. Yet all the while, the present moment slips quietly past us. We become experts in many subjects while remaining strangers to the sacredness of what is here now.

The saying suggests that spiritual perception is not merely about gathering information. It is about learning to see. The Divine is often closer than we imagine—not hidden in some distant realm, but present within the ordinary circumstances of life. Yet we overlook it because we are searching for something more dramatic, more obvious, or more certain.

To "read this moment" is a contemplative practice. It means paying attention. It means listening beneath the noise of the mind. It means asking, *What is being revealed right now?* The present moment is never empty. It is filled with invitations, lessons, beauty, and grace. But these gifts are only visible when we are awake enough to receive them.

Perhaps the deepest irony is that the people are asking Jesus to reveal himself while he is already revealed before them. The same pattern can appear in our own lives. We ask where God is while overlooking the breath in our lungs, the kindness of a friend, the beauty of a sunset, or the quiet presence available in silence. We seek signs while standing in the midst of them.

The invitation today is simple: slow down and read this moment. Not yesterday. Not tomorrow. This moment. The sacred may be much nearer than you think. It may already be waiting in the ordinary details of the day, asking only to be noticed.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Open my eyes to this moment*
>
> Exhale: *Help me see what is here*
