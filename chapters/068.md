## 68 - The Freedom of Not Belonging

> *Jesus said, "Blessed are you when you are hated and persecuted. Wherever you have been persecuted they will find no place."*

At first glance, this saying seems difficult. No one seeks hatred or persecution. No one desires rejection. Yet Jesus speaks of a blessing hidden within these painful experiences. As with many of his sayings, the blessing is not found in the suffering itself, but in what suffering can reveal.

Human beings naturally long for acceptance. We want to belong. We want to be understood. We want others to affirm who we are. These desires are deeply human and often beautiful. Yet there comes a point on every authentic spiritual journey when we discover that living from truth cannot always be reconciled with pleasing everyone around us.

When we begin to follow the deepest promptings of the soul, some people may not understand. Old expectations may be challenged. Familiar roles may no longer fit. Those who knew us in one season may struggle to recognize us in another. Growth sometimes creates tension because transformation changes the way we move through the world.

The blessing Jesus points toward may be a kind of inner freedom. When our identity is no longer dependent upon universal approval, we become less controlled by praise and blame alike. We are able to act from conviction rather than from the constant need to be accepted. We become rooted in a deeper center.

The final phrase carries a mysterious promise: *"Wherever you have been persecuted they will find no place."* Perhaps this suggests that hostility cannot ultimately take root in a heart grounded in the Kingdom. The forces of fear, judgment, and rejection lose their power when they can no longer determine our sense of worth. What once wounded us finds no permanent home within us.

This does not mean becoming hardened or indifferent. Quite the opposite. The more deeply rooted we become in Divine Love, the more freely we can love others—even when they misunderstand us. We no longer need to defend ourselves constantly because our identity rests in something deeper than public opinion.

Today, consider where you may be seeking approval at the expense of truth. Notice any fear of rejection that keeps you from living authentically. Then gently return to the deeper ground beneath those fears. You do not need everyone to understand your journey in order to walk it faithfully. The Kingdom within you is a steadier home than the changing judgments of the world.
