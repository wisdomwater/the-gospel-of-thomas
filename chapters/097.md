## 97 - The Empty Jar

> *Jesus said, "The kingdom of the father is like a certain woman who was carrying a jar full of meal. While she was walking on the road, still some distance from home, the handle of the jar broke and the meal emptied out behind her on the road. She did not realize it; she had noticed no accident. When she reached her house, she set the jar down and found it empty."*

This parable begins with abundance. A woman carries a jar full of meal, confident that what she possesses will arrive safely at its destination. Yet along the journey, something breaks. Little by little, what she carries slips away. She continues walking, unaware that the jar is gradually emptying.

Many of us know this experience, though not always in such a visible form. We move through life carrying plans, expectations, ambitions, and assumptions about what will satisfy us. We imagine a future moment when we will finally arrive and discover fulfillment. Yet somewhere along the road, things begin to fall away. Dreams change. Certainties loosen. What once seemed essential quietly slips through our fingers.

What is striking is that the woman does not notice the loss until she reaches home. Sometimes awareness arrives only after a long journey. We look back and realize that an identity we depended upon has vanished. A desire that once drove us no longer holds the same power. A version of ourselves we thought we would always be has disappeared along the way.

At first, such discoveries can feel like disappointment. We reach the destination expecting fullness and find emptiness. Yet many contemplative traditions speak of a sacred kind of emptiness. There is an emptiness that comes from loss, but there is also an emptiness that creates space. The jar that has been emptied is no longer occupied by what it once contained.

Perhaps this is why Jesus compares the Kingdom to this strange story. The spiritual life often involves a gradual emptying that we do not fully understand while it is happening. The ego's attachments, ambitions, and illusions slowly leak away. We may not even notice the transformation until we arrive at a moment of clarity and discover that we are no longer carrying what once seemed so important.

The invitation today is not to fear the empty jar. What feels like loss may be preparing the way for a deeper fullness. The Kingdom often arrives not when our hands are full, but when they have finally been opened. In the space left behind, something new becomes possible. What is emptied by life may become available to grace.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Empty me of what I do not need*
>
> Exhale: *Fill me with what endures*
