## 78 - Beyond Appearances

> *Jesus said, "Why have you come out into the desert? To see a reed shaken by the wind? And to see a man clothed in fine garments like your kings and your great men? Upon them are the fine garments, and they are unable to discern the truth."*

Jesus asks a series of questions that gently expose the motives of the heart. Why have you come? What are you truly seeking? Are you looking for something impressive, something that catches the eye, something that confirms your expectations? Or are you seeking something deeper?

The image of a reed shaken by the wind speaks of instability, of being moved by every passing current. Many of us know what it is like to live this way. We are pulled by the latest opinion, the strongest emotion, the loudest voice, or the newest distraction. The soul grows weary when it has no deeper anchor.

Then Jesus turns to those clothed in fine garments. Outward appearances can be captivating. Wealth, status, success, influence, and recognition often seem to promise wisdom or fulfillment. Yet the saying reminds us that what shines on the surface is not always where truth is found. The deepest realities of life cannot be measured by what a person possesses or displays.

The desert, throughout the spiritual tradition, is a place of simplicity. It strips away distractions and leaves us face to face with what is real. In the desert there is little to impress, little to hide behind, and little to cling to. What remains is the opportunity to encounter truth without ornament.

This invitation remains just as relevant today. We live in a world overflowing with images, personalities, and performances competing for our attention. It is easy to mistake visibility for wisdom or appearance for substance. Yet the heart knows the difference. Beneath all the noise is a quieter voice calling us toward authenticity, depth, and presence.

Perhaps Jesus' questions are meant for us as well. Why have you come? What are you seeking beneath your seeking? If you listen carefully, you may discover that what your soul desires is not another spectacle, but truth itself. And truth often arrives without fine garments, waiting quietly in the simplicity of the present moment.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Lead me beyond appearances*
>
> Exhale: *Into what is true*
