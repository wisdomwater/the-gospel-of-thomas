## 47 - Making Room for the New

> *Jesus said, "It is impossible for a man to mount two horses or to stretch two bows. And it is impossible for a servant to serve two masters; otherwise, he will honor the one and treat the other contemptuously. No man drinks old wine and immediately desires to drink new wine. And new wine is not put into old wineskins, lest they burst; nor is old wine put into a new wineskin, lest it spoil it. An old patch is not sewn onto a new garment, because a tear would result."*

This saying gathers together a series of images, yet they all seem to point toward a common truth: transformation requires wholeness. A person cannot ride in two directions at once. New life cannot simply be squeezed into old forms without consequence. Something within us must become willing to change.

Most of us know the tension of wanting renewal while remaining exactly as we are. We long for peace but cling to familiar anxieties. We seek freedom while holding tightly to old identities. We desire a deeper life, yet often hope it can arrive without disrupting the patterns we have carefully built around ourselves.

The images of wine and wineskins speak to the living nature of spiritual growth. New wine is active, expanding, alive. It cannot be contained indefinitely within structures that have become rigid. When fresh insight, deeper awareness, or a new experience of Divine Love enters our lives, it often asks something of us. It invites us to become more flexible, more open, more willing to release what no longer serves the journey.

There is no condemnation in this process. Jesus does not seem to be criticizing the old wine or the old wineskins. The old has its place and its season. Yet every stage of life brings new invitations. What once carried us faithfully may eventually become too small for what is now emerging within us.

The contemplative path is often less about adding something new and more about creating space. We loosen our grip on old certainties. We release habits that keep us asleep. We allow the heart to become spacious enough to receive what is unfolding. The Kingdom grows not through force but through openness.

Today, notice where life may be inviting you into a larger container. Is there an old story about yourself that no longer fits? A fear that has become too small for the person you are becoming? A belief that once protected you but now limits you? Gently make room. The new wine is already fermenting. Trust that the vessel can grow along with the gift.
