## 58 - Through the Wound, Into Life

> *Jesus said, "Blessed is the man who has suffered and found life."*

This saying does not say that suffering itself is blessed. Rather, it speaks of a person who has passed through suffering and discovered something precious on the other side. The blessing is not the wound. The blessing is the life that was found.

Every human being suffers. We experience loss, disappointment, illness, uncertainty, grief, and change. Some wounds arrive suddenly. Others accompany us quietly for years. Suffering is one of the few experiences that unites us all, regardless of our background or beliefs.

Yet suffering has a way of stripping away illusions. It can expose what is fragile, what is temporary, and what cannot ultimately sustain us. The things we once depended upon may fall away. The identities we carefully constructed may crack. What remains is often a deeper encounter with reality itself.

Many people emerge from hardship with a greater capacity for compassion. They become gentler with others because they understand pain from the inside. They become less interested in appearances and more interested in what is real. Their hearts gain depth. Their love becomes more spacious. In this way, suffering can become a doorway—not because pain is inherently good, but because it can awaken us to a deeper life.

The life Jesus speaks of may not be the return of everything as it once was. More often, it is the discovery of something that was hidden beneath the struggle all along. A resilience we did not know we possessed. A peace that does not depend on circumstances. A love that survives loss. A presence that remains even in darkness.

This does not mean we must seek suffering or glorify it. When possible, we alleviate suffering in ourselves and others. Yet when suffering comes, as it inevitably does, we can trust that it need not have the final word. There is life beyond despair. There is wisdom beyond confusion. There is resurrection hidden within countless endings.

Today, honor whatever wounds you carry. You do not need to rush past them or pretend they are not there. Simply hold them gently. And remember: many of the deepest wells of wisdom, compassion, and love are discovered by those who have walked through pain and found life waiting on the other side.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Through every wound*
>
> Exhale: *Lead me into life*
