## 112 - Living in Harmony

> *Jesus said, "Woe to the flesh that depends on the soul; woe to the soul that depends on the flesh."*

This saying arrives as a paradox. Jesus speaks of both flesh and soul, yet neither is meant to dominate the other. A warning is given in both directions, suggesting that something essential is lost whenever one part of our being attempts to carry the entire weight of life.

Many of us live from imbalance without realizing it. Sometimes we become consumed by physical concerns—comfort, achievement, appearance, security, and endless activity. At other times, we retreat into ideas, beliefs, and spiritual aspirations while neglecting the practical realities of embodied life. The soul drifts away from the earth, or the body forgets its connection to the deeper life within.

The wisdom of this saying may be found in the invitation to wholeness. We are neither merely flesh nor merely soul. We are living beings in whom the visible and invisible meet. Our spiritual lives are not meant to reject the body, nor are our physical lives meant to eclipse the life of the spirit. Both belong together.

Consider a tree. Its branches reach toward the sky while its roots sink deep into the earth. If either is neglected, the tree suffers. In the same way, we flourish when our inner and outer lives support one another. The body becomes a vessel through which love is expressed. The soul becomes the living breath that animates our actions with meaning and purpose.

When we cultivate this harmony, ordinary life itself becomes sacred. Eating, resting, working, creating, serving, and praying are no longer separate compartments. They become expressions of a unified life. The soul is present in the body, and the body participates in the life of the soul. What once felt divided begins to move together.

Perhaps the warning in this saying is ultimately a call to integration. Whenever we place the burden of our entire identity upon one aspect of ourselves, we lose touch with the fullness of who we are. But when we honor both the inward and outward dimensions of life, we discover a deeper balance and freedom.

Today, notice whether there is a part of yourself that has been neglected. Is your body asking for care, rest, or attention? Is your soul longing for silence, beauty, or prayer? Listen gently. The sacred often meets us where these dimensions of our lives come back into harmony.
