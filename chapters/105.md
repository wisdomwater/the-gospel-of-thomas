## 105 - Beyond the Labels

> *Jesus said, "He who knows the father and the mother will be called the son of a harlot."*

This saying arrives like a riddle. It seems almost designed to unsettle us. One moment it speaks of knowing the father and the mother, and the next it speaks of being given a harsh and unexpected name. As with many paradoxes, its purpose may not be to provide an easy answer but to invite us beyond our usual ways of seeing.

Most of us spend much of our lives living within labels. We learn who we are through the names, expectations, and identities given to us by others. Some labels encourage us. Others wound us. Yet all of them can become limitations if we begin to mistake them for our deepest self.

There are moments on the spiritual journey when a person begins to see more clearly. Old assumptions fall away. The divisions that once seemed obvious become less certain. The soul discovers a deeper ground beneath inherited identities. This kind of awakening is often misunderstood. Those who step beyond familiar boundaries may find that others do not know what to make of them.

The saying suggests that genuine knowing may come with a cost. To see beyond appearances is not always comfortable. To recognize the sacred mystery that gives rise to all life may place us outside the categories by which people measure worth and belonging. We may be misunderstood, questioned, or even judged. Yet the invitation is to remain faithful to what has been seen.

Perhaps the father and the mother symbolize the fullness of reality itself—the source from which all things emerge and the nurturing presence that sustains them. To know both is to encounter a wholeness that transcends simple divisions. Such knowledge cannot be contained by conventional labels.

The good news is that our true identity does not depend upon the names others give us. Beneath praise and criticism, acceptance and rejection, there remains something deeper and more enduring. The invitation of the spiritual path is to rest there. When we live from that deeper center, we become less concerned with how we are perceived and more attentive to the quiet truth unfolding within.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Ground me in truth*
>
> Exhale: *Beyond every label*
