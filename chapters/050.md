## 50 - Children of the Light

> *Jesus said, "If they say to you, 'Where did you come from?', say to them, 'We came from the light, the place where the light came into being on its own accord and established itself and became manifest through their image.' If they say to you, 'Is it you?', say, 'We are its children, we are the elect of the living father.' If they ask you, 'What is the sign of your father in you?', say to them, 'It is movement and repose.'"*

This saying feels like a poem spoken from a deeper place than ordinary language. It asks questions about identity, origin, and belonging. Where did you come from? Who are you? What is the sign of the Divine within you? These are questions that every human heart encounters sooner or later.

Jesus begins with a surprising answer: *"We came from the light."* Before our achievements, failures, roles, and stories, there is something more fundamental about who we are. At the deepest level, we arise from a sacred source. The light spoken of here may be understood as the life of God, the presence of Divine Love, or the mystery from which all existence emerges.

Yet most of us spend much of our lives forgetting this. We define ourselves by our circumstances, our wounds, our successes, or the opinions of others. We become entangled in identities that are real but incomplete. This saying gently invites us to remember a deeper identity—not something we must earn, but something we already possess.

Jesus then speaks of being children of the living Father. A child does not create its inheritance. It receives it. In the same way, the spiritual journey may be less about becoming worthy of divine love and more about awakening to the love that has always held us. The light is not distant from us. It shines through us, even when we are unaware of it.

The final image is perhaps the most beautiful: *"movement and repose."* Life contains both. There are seasons of action and seasons of stillness, times of growth and times of rest, moments when we are called forward and moments when we are invited to be still. The sign of the Divine is not found in endless striving or perpetual inactivity, but in the harmony between the two.

Perhaps this is what a mature spiritual life looks like. We move when it is time to move. We rest when it is time to rest. We engage the world without losing our center. We become active without becoming frantic and still without becoming stagnant. Like the breath itself, life flows in and out, movement and repose held together in a single rhythm.

Today, remember where you come from. Beneath every label and every passing circumstance, you are a child of the light. Let that remembrance accompany you in both your activity and your stillness. The same sacred presence lives within them both.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *I come from the Light*
>
> Exhale: *I rest in the Light*
