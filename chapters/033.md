## 33 - Let the Light Be Seen

> *Jesus said, "Preach from your housetops that which you will hear in your ear. For no one lights a lamp and puts it under a bushel, nor does he put it in a hidden place, but rather he sets it on a lamp stand so that everyone who enters and leaves will see its light."*

This saying begins in quietness and ends in radiance. First there is something heard in the ear—a whisper, a truth, an insight received in stillness. Then there is a lamp placed where its light can shine. Jesus reminds us that what is genuinely received in the depths of the soul is not meant to remain buried.

Many of the most important truths arrive quietly. They come during prayer, in moments of reflection, on a solitary walk, or in the midst of an ordinary day. They rarely arrive with fanfare. More often they appear as a gentle knowing, a subtle shift in perspective, or a deep sense of peace. The sacred frequently speaks in whispers.

Yet there is a difference between receiving light and hiding it. Sometimes we keep our deepest insights locked away because we fear misunderstanding, rejection, or vulnerability. At other times, we simply forget to embody what we have learned. The light remains real, but it never fully reaches the world around us.

Jesus uses the image of a lamp to illustrate a simple truth: light exists to illuminate. The purpose of wisdom is not merely personal enrichment. The purpose of love is not private enjoyment. The purpose of awakening is not self-congratulation. Whatever light we receive is meant to become a blessing for others.

This does not necessarily mean standing on literal rooftops or making grand proclamations. Most often, the light shines through the ordinary moments of life. It appears in a patient response when irritation would be easier. It appears in a compassionate word, a listening ear, a forgiving heart, or a quiet act of kindness. The brightest lamps are often found in the simplest places.

The paradox is that we do not create the light by trying to shine. We simply stop hiding it. As we become more authentic, more present, and more aligned with the truth we have received, the light naturally reveals itself. Others may not remember our words, but they often remember how they felt in our presence.

Today, consider what light has been entrusted to you. What truth have you learned? What kindness have you received? What wisdom has quietly taken root within your heart? Do not be afraid to let it shine. The world does not need perfection. It needs people willing to share the light they have been given.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Kindle Your light within me*
>
> Exhale: *May it shine for others*
