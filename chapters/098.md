## 98 - The Courage to Begin

> *Jesus said, "The kingdom of the father is like a certain man who wanted to kill a powerful man. In his own house he drew his sword and stuck it into the wall in order to find out whether his hand could carry through. Then he slew the powerful man."*

This is one of the more surprising images in the Gospel of Thomas. A man prepares to confront someone powerful. Before he does, he tests his strength. In private, away from the moment of confrontation, he practices the movement he will later need. Only then does he face what once seemed beyond him.

Read contemplatively, the "powerful man" need not be another person. We all encounter powerful forces within ourselves—fear, resentment, pride, despair, old habits, and deeply rooted patterns that seem larger than our will. These inner powers can shape our lives for years, convincing us that change is impossible.

Yet transformation rarely begins in the dramatic moment. It begins quietly, often unseen. The man first practices in his own house. Likewise, spiritual growth often starts in small, hidden choices. A moment of patience when irritation arises. A few minutes of silence each morning. A willingness to forgive a small hurt. These humble acts may seem insignificant, yet they are preparing the heart for larger transformations.

Many of us wait until we feel completely ready before taking a step forward. We imagine that courage arrives first and action follows. More often, the opposite is true. Courage grows through practice. Strength develops through use. Faith deepens through small acts of trust repeated over time.

The Kingdom, in this saying, is not merely a gift received; it is also a life cultivated. God works within us, yet we are invited to participate. The habits of awareness, compassion, forgiveness, and presence gradually strengthen the soul. What once felt impossible begins to feel natural.

Perhaps there is something in your life that appears powerful and unmovable. An old wound. A persistent fear. A pattern that keeps returning. Rather than trying to conquer it all at once, consider what small practice might prepare you for the journey ahead. What quiet act of faithfulness can you undertake today?

The Kingdom often grows in hidden places. Long before the visible victory, there is the unseen preparation. Long before the breakthrough, there is the willingness to begin. And sometimes the smallest act of courage becomes the first movement toward freedom.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Strengthen my heart for the journey*
>
> Exhale: *Help me begin with courage*
