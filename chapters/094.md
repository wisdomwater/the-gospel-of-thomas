## 94 - Keep Knocking

> *Jesus said, "He who seeks will find, and he who knocks will be let in."*

These words are simple enough for a child to understand and deep enough to accompany a lifetime of contemplation. Jesus speaks of seeking and knocking—two actions that require movement, intention, and hope. They assume that the journey is not finished and that there is something worth pursuing.

To seek is to admit that we do not yet see the whole picture. It is an act of humility. The seeker acknowledges that there is more to learn, more to discover, more to awaken to. Yet seeking is also an act of trust. We do not search unless we believe, however faintly, that something meaningful can be found.

The same is true of knocking. We knock on a door because we believe someone may answer. We approach the threshold because we sense there is something beyond it. Every prayer, every moment of silence, every honest question, and every act of attention can become a kind of knock upon the door of mystery.

What is remarkable about this saying is its confidence. Jesus does not say that the seeker *might* find or that the one who knocks *may* be admitted. The language is hopeful and direct. The path may not unfold according to our timetable, and what we find may not be what we expected, but the sincere search is not wasted.

Often we begin by seeking answers, yet along the way we discover something deeper. We seek certainty and find trust. We seek understanding and find wonder. We seek God and find that God has been quietly present all along. The door we imagined to be locked may have been waiting for us to notice it.

The contemplative life is not about striving anxiously for a distant goal. It is about remaining open, curious, and available. It is about continuing to seek even when the way is unclear and continuing to knock even when the answer has not yet come. The seeking itself shapes the heart. The knocking itself awakens expectation.

Perhaps the invitation today is simply not to stop. If there is a longing in your soul, honor it. If there is a question that continues to return, listen to it. If there is a door that calls for your attention, approach it. The journey of awakening often begins with a single step and continues with a thousand small acts of trust.

Keep seeking. Keep knocking. The path is already opening beneath your feet.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *I seek with an open heart*
>
> Exhale: *I trust the door will open*
