## 20 - The Smallest Seed

> *The disciples said to Jesus, "Tell us what the kingdom of heaven is like." He said to them, "It is like a mustard seed. It is the smallest of all seeds. But when it falls on tilled soil, it produces a great plant and becomes a shelter for birds of the sky."*

When we imagine the kingdom of heaven, we often expect something grand and unmistakable. We look for dramatic signs, extraordinary experiences, or sudden transformations. Yet Jesus points to something remarkably small—a seed so tiny it could easily be overlooked.

There is wisdom in this image. Much of what is most important in life begins quietly. A friendship begins with a conversation. A healing journey begins with a single honest moment. A life of prayer begins with a few minutes of stillness. The most profound transformations often arrive disguised as small beginnings.

The mustard seed reminds us not to despise what seems insignificant. A moment of kindness, a word of forgiveness, a choice to be present rather than distracted—these may appear small, yet they carry within them the potential for far more than we can see. The kingdom grows in hidden ways, beneath the surface, long before it becomes visible.

Jesus also speaks of tilled soil. Seeds do not flourish on hard ground. In the same way, the heart must remain open and receptive. The soil of the soul is softened through humility, surrender, curiosity, and love. When we loosen our grip on certainty and make space for the sacred, something new is able to take root within us.

Over time, the tiny seed becomes a shelter for birds of the sky. What begins as a private transformation gradually becomes a blessing for others. Peace spreads. Compassion deepens. Wisdom matures. The kingdom is never meant to remain locked inside us. It grows until it offers refuge, encouragement, and life to those around us.

Today, pay attention to the small seeds already present in your life. Perhaps they seem too ordinary to matter. Perhaps they appear unfinished or fragile. Trust them anyway. The kingdom often enters quietly, hidden within the smallest acts of love. Given time, care, and openness, what seems insignificant today may become a place of shelter and blessing tomorrow.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Plant Your kingdom within me*

> Exhale: *Help it grow in love*
