## 92 - The Question Not Yet Asked

> *Jesus said, "Seek and you will find. Yet, what you asked me about in former times and which I did not tell you then, now I do desire to tell, but you do not inquire after it."*

Jesus begins with a promise: *Seek and you will find.* These words affirm the sincerity of the spiritual journey. The heart that genuinely longs for truth is not ignored. The path may be winding, and the answers may arrive in unexpected forms, but the search itself is meaningful.

Then the saying takes an unexpected turn. Jesus speaks of questions that were once asked but not answered. Now, he says, the time has come when he desires to reveal what was hidden—but the questions have stopped. Something has changed, not in the willingness of the teacher, but in the attention of the seeker.

There are seasons in life when certain truths cannot yet be received. We may ask the right questions, but our understanding is not ready. Wisdom often unfolds according to a deeper rhythm than our curiosity would prefer. Looking back, many of us can see moments when an answer given too soon would have made little sense. Life itself had to prepare the soil before the seed could take root.

Yet there is another challenge. Sometimes we stop asking. We become distracted by lesser concerns. We settle into routines. The urgent crowds out the important. The questions that once stirred our souls are quietly forgotten beneath the demands of everyday life.

The contemplative path invites us to recover our capacity for wonder. Not every question is equal. Some questions arise from curiosity alone, while others emerge from the deepest places within us. *Who am I? What is truly real? What does love require of me? Where is God in this moment?* Such questions have the power to transform a life because they keep the heart open.

Perhaps Jesus is suggesting that revelation is often waiting closer than we imagine. The Divine is not withholding truth out of reluctance. The invitation remains. But are we listening? Are we asking the questions that matter most? Have we become so occupied with answers that we have forgotten how to seek?

Today, consider what question is alive within your soul. Not the question others expect you to ask, but the one that quietly returns in moments of silence. Bring it gently into the light. The answer may not arrive immediately, but the act of asking may itself become a doorway into deeper awareness.

**Breath Prayer**
