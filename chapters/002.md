## 2 - The Holy Disturbance

> *Jesus said, "Let him who seeks continue seeking until he finds. When he finds, he will become troubled. When he becomes troubled, he will be astonished, and he will rule over the All."*

This saying unfolds like a journey through unexpected terrain. We might imagine that seeking ends in certainty, comfort, or easy answers. Yet Jesus describes a different path. The seeker finds something, but instead of immediate peace, there is first a holy disturbance. Something shifts. Old assumptions no longer fit. Familiar ways of seeing begin to crack open.

Most of us have known moments like this. A truth becomes visible that we can no longer ignore. A deeper understanding emerges that changes how we see ourselves, others, or God. What once seemed solid suddenly feels incomplete. Such moments can be unsettling because they invite us beyond the boundaries of the world we have carefully constructed.

Yet the trouble spoken of here is not punishment. It is the discomfort of awakening. It is the feeling of standing at the edge of a larger horizon. When deeper reality begins to reveal itself, the ego often trembles because it senses that it is no longer the center of the story. Something greater is calling us forward.

If we remain present through that discomfort, astonishment awaits us. We begin to see life with new eyes. The sacred appears where we once saw only ordinary moments. The divine presence that seemed distant is discovered woven through every breath, every encounter, every corner of creation. Wonder becomes less an occasional experience and more a way of seeing.

The final words of the saying point toward a profound freedom. To "rule over the All" need not suggest domination but participation. We are no longer ruled by fear, illusion, or the restless need to control. We become grounded in a deeper center. Life still contains challenges, but we meet them from a place of spaciousness rather than reactivity.

The invitation is simple: keep seeking. Do not fear the questions that unsettle you. Do not rush past the moments that disturb your certainty. Sometimes what feels like disorientation is actually the doorway to wonder. The path may lead through trouble, but it also leads toward a freedom wider than you can presently imagine.
