## 84 - Meeting the Deeper Self

> *Jesus said, "When you see your likeness, you rejoice. But when you see your images which came into being before you, and which neither die nor become manifest, how much you will have to bear!"*

It is natural to rejoice when we see our likeness. We recognize ourselves in a photograph, a reflection, a memory, or a story. There is comfort in familiarity. We know the face we present to the world. We know the identity we have carried through the years. We know the narrative that tells us who we are.

Yet Jesus points toward something deeper and more mysterious. Beyond the likeness we readily recognize are "images" that came before us, realities that cannot be seen in the ordinary way and do not pass away as outward forms do. The saying invites us to consider that there is more to our identity than the self we have learned to describe.

The contemplative journey often begins with self-discovery, but it rarely ends there. At first, we become aware of our habits, desires, fears, strengths, and wounds. This awareness is valuable. Yet eventually we encounter a deeper mystery: the realization that our truest self cannot be fully captured by any description. There is something within us that existed before every role we assumed and remains untouched by every success or failure.

Why does Jesus say that seeing these deeper images is something we will have to bear? Perhaps because such seeing can be overwhelming. It challenges the small identities we have carefully constructed. It reveals a depth we cannot control and a mystery we cannot fully explain. To glimpse the eternal within ourselves is both beautiful and unsettling. It stretches our understanding beyond familiar boundaries.

There are moments when this deeper reality breaks through. We may experience it in silence, in prayer, in wonder, in profound love, or in a sudden sense of connection to something greater than ourselves. For an instant, we recognize that we are more than our history and larger than our fears. Such moments can feel both exhilarating and humbling.

The invitation of this saying is not to solve the mystery but to remain open to it. You are more than the image in the mirror. More than the story you tell about yourself. More than the collection of experiences that have shaped your life. Beneath all of these is a deeper reality, held in God, shining quietly through your existence. To encounter it may require courage, but it is also the beginning of freedom.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Reveal my deeper self*
>
> Exhale: *Help me rest in mystery*
