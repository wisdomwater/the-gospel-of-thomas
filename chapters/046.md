## 46 - The Wisdom of Becoming Small

> *Jesus said, "Among those born of women, from Adam until John the Baptist, there is no one so superior to John the Baptist that his eyes should not be lowered before him. Yet I have said, whichever one of you comes to be a child will be acquainted with the kingdom and will become superior to John."*

This saying begins with greatness and ends with childhood. Jesus honors John the Baptist as one of the greatest figures to walk the earth, yet then turns expectations upside down. The doorway into the Kingdom is not found through status, achievement, knowledge, or spiritual accomplishment. It is found through becoming like a child.

A child meets the world differently than most adults do. Children are naturally curious. They wonder. They trust. They ask questions without embarrassment. They can become completely absorbed in a moment, finding delight in things that adults often overlook. Their attention has not yet become hardened by certainty.

As we move through life, we often trade wonder for expertise. We learn to categorize, evaluate, and control. While these abilities have their place, they can slowly distance us from the freshness of direct experience. We stop seeing what is actually before us because we believe we already know what it is.

Perhaps this is why Jesus points toward childhood. The Kingdom is not merely something to be understood. It is something to be encountered. It reveals itself to hearts that remain open, receptive, and awake. The childlike soul is willing to be surprised by grace. It is willing to stand in mystery without demanding immediate answers.

To become a child is not to become immature. It is to recover a deeper innocence beneath our defenses. It is to loosen our grip on the need to appear wise, successful, or certain. It is to approach life once again as a gift rather than a problem to be solved.

Today, allow yourself to see with new eyes. Notice the light through a window, the sound of birds, the warmth of a cup in your hands. Let yourself be curious about your own life. The Kingdom may be closer than you think, quietly waiting where wonder and presence meet.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Open me to wonder*
>
> Exhale: *Teach me to receive*
