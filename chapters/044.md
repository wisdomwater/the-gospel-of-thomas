## 44 - Resisting the Living Breath

> *Jesus said, "Whoever blasphemes against the father will be forgiven, and whoever blasphemes against the son will be forgiven, but whoever blasphemes against the holy spirit will not be forgiven either on earth or in heaven."*

At first glance, these words can feel unsettling. They seem to speak of a boundary that cannot be crossed, a wound that cannot be healed. Yet perhaps this saying invites us to look beyond punishment and toward something deeper: our relationship with the living presence of God within and around us.

We can misunderstand ideas. We can reject teachings. We can struggle with images of God that no longer speak to us. Much of spiritual growth involves questioning, wrestling, and even letting old understandings fall away. There is grace for this. The journey of faith is rarely a straight line, and sincere seeking often passes through uncertainty.

But the Holy Spirit has often been understood as the living breath of God—the immediate presence that awakens, guides, and illuminates. If this is so, then to reject that inner light is different from rejecting an idea. It is to continually turn away from the very source of healing itself. The problem is not that God refuses forgiveness. The problem is that we close ourselves to receiving what is already being offered.

We experience this in ordinary life. No one can force another person to awaken. No one can make us see what we are determined not to see. When we continually harden ourselves against truth, compassion, or love, we remain trapped not because grace has disappeared, but because we have shut the door through which grace enters.

Yet even this saying need not be read with fear. The fact that we are concerned about our openness to the Spirit may itself be evidence that the door remains open. The living breath of God is patient. It whispers rather than shouts. It waits beneath our resistance, inviting us again and again into greater honesty, greater awareness, and greater love.

Perhaps the invitation today is simple: do not resist what is alive within you. When a deeper truth arises, welcome it. When love calls you beyond old habits, listen. When the quiet voice of wisdom speaks beneath the noise, make room for it. The Spirit is not something distant to be believed in. It is the sacred breath moving through this very moment, gently inviting you home.
