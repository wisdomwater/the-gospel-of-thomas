## 60 - A Place of Repose

> *They saw a Samaritan carrying a lamb on his way to Judea. He said to his disciples, "That man is round about the lamb."*
>
> *They said to him, "So that he may kill it and eat it."*
>
> *He said to them, "While it is alive, he will not eat it, but only when he has killed it and it has become a corpse."*
>
> *They said to him, "He cannot do so otherwise."*
>
> *He said to them, "You too, look for a place for yourself within repose, lest you become a corpse and be eaten."*

This saying unfolds like a riddle. A man carries a lamb. The disciples offer a practical explanation. Jesus then turns the conversation inward, transforming an ordinary scene into a spiritual invitation. As often happens in the Gospel of Thomas, what begins in the visible world becomes a doorway into the hidden life of the soul.

The striking image at the end is the one that lingers: *"Look for a place for yourself within repose."* Repose is more than physical rest. It is a state of deep inner stillness. It is the quiet center within us that remains untouched by the endless movement of thoughts, fears, ambitions, and distractions. It is the place where the soul remembers its home in God.

Many people move through life without ever finding this place. We become consumed by activity, worry, achievement, and reaction. Our days are filled with motion, yet something within us gradually falls asleep. We may continue functioning outwardly, but inwardly we become disconnected from the living source of our being. In this sense, we become like the "corpse" of the saying—existing, but not fully alive.

Jesus seems to be warning against this kind of spiritual numbness. A life disconnected from awareness can easily be consumed by the demands of the world. Anxiety eats at our peace. Resentment eats at our joy. Endless striving eats at our capacity for wonder. We become food for the very forces that keep us from living deeply.

Yet there is another possibility. We can intentionally seek repose. We can cultivate moments of silence. We can return to the breath, to prayer, to stillness, to the simple awareness of God's presence. In doing so, we discover a place within that cannot be consumed by fear or circumstance. We become rooted in something deeper than the changing conditions of life.

Perhaps this is what Jesus is inviting us toward: not escape from the world, but a center within the world. A place of stillness that travels with us wherever we go. A place where life remains alive and awake. A place where the soul can rest in the presence of the Living One.

Today, pause for a few moments. Let the noise settle. Let your attention sink beneath the surface of your thoughts. There, in the quiet depths, is a place of repose waiting to receive you. Return to it often. It is one of the ways we remain truly alive.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Lead me into stillness*
>
> Exhale: *I rest in You*
