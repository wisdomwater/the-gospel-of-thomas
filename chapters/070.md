## 70 - Bring Forth What Is Within You

> *Jesus said, "That which you have will save you if you bring it forth from yourselves. That which you do not have within you will kill you if you do not have it within you."*

This saying speaks with the language of paradox, yet its invitation is deeply personal. Jesus points not to something outside of us, but to something already present within. There is a treasure hidden in the depths of the soul, and the task of the spiritual life is not merely to acquire it, but to uncover and express it.

Many of us spend our lives looking elsewhere for what we most need. We search for wisdom in books, validation from others, security through possessions, or peace through changing circumstances. These things can be valuable, yet Jesus seems to suggest that the deepest source of life is already nearer than we realize.

Within each person lies a capacity for love, compassion, awareness, and communion with the Divine. There is a light that often remains hidden beneath fear, distraction, old wounds, and false identities. When this deeper life is brought forth—when it is expressed through our words, actions, and way of being—it becomes a source of healing and freedom.

The second half of the saying offers the other side of the invitation. What remains undeveloped, unacknowledged, or disconnected within us can become a source of suffering. A heart that never learns to love becomes imprisoned by bitterness. A soul that never awakens to its deeper nature remains trapped in endless striving. The neglected parts of our spiritual life eventually ask for our attention.

Yet this is not a threat. It is an encouragement. The seed already exists. The possibility of transformation is already present. We do not need to manufacture the Kingdom. We need only allow what has been planted within us to grow and emerge into the light.

The contemplative path is one way of nurturing this emergence. In silence, we become aware of the deeper currents beneath our thoughts. In prayer, we learn to trust the presence already dwelling within us. In acts of love, we give expression to what is most true. Slowly, the hidden treasure becomes visible.

Today, consider what beautiful thing may be waiting to emerge from within you. Perhaps it is kindness. Perhaps courage. Perhaps forgiveness. Perhaps a deeper trust in life itself. Whatever it is, do not keep it buried. Bring it forth gently. The gift you carry is not meant to remain hidden. It is meant to become part of the healing of the world.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Awaken what is within me*
>
> Exhale: *Bring it into the light*
