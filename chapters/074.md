## 74 - Thirst Beside an Empty Well

> *He said, "O Lord, there are many around the drinking trough, but there is nothing in the cistern."*

The image is striking. A crowd gathers around a place where water should be found. The trough is there. The people are there. Yet the source itself is empty. Everyone waits beside a promise that cannot satisfy their thirst.

This saying speaks to a familiar human experience. We often gather around things we hope will nourish us—success, possessions, recognition, certainty, distraction, even religious routines. We return again and again, expecting fulfillment, only to discover that something essential is missing. The form remains, but the living water is absent.

There is a difference between being near water and actually drinking. We can spend years circling around spiritual things without allowing them to reach the depths of our hearts. We can collect ideas about God while remaining strangers to stillness. We can speak of love while neglecting to receive it. The soul recognizes the difference, even when the mind does not.

The saying also invites compassion. The people gathered at the trough are not foolish; they are thirsty. Every restless pursuit, every grasping for more, every anxious search for fulfillment is often the expression of a deeper longing. Beneath our endless striving lives a sincere desire for what is real, lasting, and life-giving.

The contemplative path gently redirects our attention from the trough to the source. Rather than asking what we can acquire, we begin asking what truly nourishes. Rather than chasing every promise of satisfaction, we learn to sit quietly with our thirst until it leads us deeper. Sometimes the recognition that the cistern is empty is itself a gift, for it frees us to seek living water elsewhere.

Perhaps today's invitation is simply to notice where you are looking for fulfillment. Is the source nourishing your spirit, or only occupying your attention? There is no shame in discovering an empty cistern. Such discoveries often become the beginning of wisdom. The thirst you carry is not a problem to be solved but a guide leading you toward something deeper than you have yet imagined.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Lead me to living water*
>
> Exhale: *Quench my deepest thirst*
