## 42 - Traveling Light

> *Jesus said, "Become passers-by."*

These words are remarkably simple, yet they carry a surprising depth. A passer-by does not cling to every place he visits. He does not attempt to possess the landscape or control the path beneath his feet. He moves through the world with open hands, receiving what is given and releasing what is not his to keep.

Many of our struggles arise from the belief that we must hold tightly to everything. We cling to opinions, identities, disappointments, achievements, and fears. We build homes within passing moments and then suffer when those moments inevitably change. Yet life itself is always moving. Every season arrives, stays for a time, and then gives way to another.

To become a passer-by is not to withdraw from life. It is not indifference or detachment from love. Rather, it is a way of participating fully without becoming imprisoned by what is temporary. We can love deeply while remembering that every experience is part of a larger journey. We can appreciate the beauty of the present moment without demanding that it remain forever.

There is a quiet freedom that emerges when we stop trying to possess what was never ours to own. The passing cloud, the changing body, the shifting emotions, the praise of others, and even our most cherished plans all belong to the flowing movement of life. We suffer less when we learn to greet them with gratitude and let them continue on their way.

Perhaps Jesus is inviting us to walk through the world more lightly. Not hurriedly, not carelessly, but with a gentle awareness that this moment is a gift rather than a possession. We are travelers in a sacred landscape. The Kingdom is not something we capture and keep; it is something we awaken to again and again as we move through each day.

Today, notice what you are carrying. Notice what you are gripping with clenched hands. Then, if only for a moment, imagine loosening your hold. Become a passer-by. Let life flow. Let love remain. Let your heart be free enough to continue the journey.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *I walk lightly*
>
> Exhale: *I release my grasp*
