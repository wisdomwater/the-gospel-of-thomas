## 38 - When the Silence Comes

> *Jesus said, "Many times have you desired to hear these words which I am saying to you, and you have no one else to hear them from. There will be days when you will look for me and will not find me."*

There is both tenderness and poignancy in this saying. Jesus acknowledges the longing of his disciples. They have hungered for words of life, for wisdom, for the presence that speaks directly to the heart. Yet he also prepares them for a time when that presence will seem absent, when they will seek and not immediately find.

Most spiritual journeys include such seasons. There are times when the sacred feels close and unmistakable. Prayer flows naturally. Insight arises easily. The heart feels warmed by a sense of connection. Then there are other seasons when the heavens seem silent. The practices that once nourished us feel dry. The presence we once sensed so clearly appears hidden.

When these times arrive, it is tempting to assume that something has gone wrong. We may think we have failed, wandered off course, or somehow lost what was once given. Yet many contemplatives have observed that absence can become its own kind of teacher. The silence is not always abandonment. Sometimes it is an invitation to deepen.

When we can no longer rely upon familiar feelings, we are invited to discover a more mature trust. We begin to love the sacred not only when it is easy to feel, but also when it is hidden. We learn to remain faithful to the journey even when certainty disappears. In doing so, our roots grow deeper than emotion alone.

There is another gift hidden in these seasons. When the outward voice grows quiet, we may begin to hear more carefully. We discover that the wisdom we sought outside ourselves has already taken root within. The teachings we once received become living realities. The words spoken long ago continue to unfold in the heart.

The saying reminds us to cherish the moments of insight while they are present. Every season of illumination is precious. Yet it also reassures us that the journey does not end when the light seems to fade. The sacred remains present even when it cannot be easily perceived. The sun has not disappeared simply because clouds have gathered.

Today, if you find yourself in a season of clarity, receive it with gratitude. If you find yourself in a season of silence, receive that too with gentleness. Continue walking. Continue listening. The One you seek is often closer than you realize, working quietly beneath the surface until the time comes for new understanding to emerge.
