## 34 - Learning to See

> *Jesus said, "If a blind man leads a blind man, they will both fall into a pit."*

This saying is brief, yet it carries a profound invitation. At first, it appears to be a warning about leadership and guidance. But beneath the surface lies a deeper question: What does it mean to truly see?

Most of us move through life believing we see clearly. We have opinions, assumptions, convictions, and habits of interpretation. Yet much of what we call seeing is filtered through fear, desire, memory, and expectation. We do not simply perceive reality as it is; we often perceive reality as we have learned to see it.

The blindness Jesus speaks of need not be understood as ignorance alone. It can also be unconsciousness. We may be driven by patterns we have never examined. We may follow beliefs we have inherited without reflection. We may chase goals that promise fulfillment while never asking whether they lead where we truly wish to go.

There is a temptation to place our trust entirely in external guides. Teachers, leaders, traditions, books, and communities all have value. Yet none can substitute for awakening within our own hearts. The spiritual path ultimately calls us to become conscious participants in our journey rather than passive followers of someone else's vision.

The good news is that sight can grow. Every moment of honest self-reflection opens our eyes a little wider. Every act of humility helps us recognize what we do not yet know. Every experience of silence and contemplation teaches us to look beneath appearances. True seeing is less about possessing certainty and more about becoming increasingly awake.

The saying also invites compassion. We are all blind in some ways. We all have places where our vision is incomplete. The goal is not to judge ourselves or others for this condition. The goal is to seek greater awareness together, encouraging one another toward deeper clarity and wisdom.

Today, pause and ask yourself: Where might I be moving through life on autopilot? What assumptions have I never questioned? What truths might I be overlooking? Hold these questions gently. The first step toward sight is often the willingness to admit that there is more to see.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Open the eyes of my heart*

> Exhale: *Lead me into deeper seeing*
