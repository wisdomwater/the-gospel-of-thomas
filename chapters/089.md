## 89 - The Inside and the Outside

> *Jesus said, "Why do you wash the outside of the cup? Do you not realize that he who made the inside is the same one who made the outside?"*

A cup may appear spotless on the outside while remaining neglected within. Jesus uses this simple image to point toward a deeper truth about the human condition. We naturally pay attention to what can be seen. We care about appearances, impressions, achievements, and the ways we are perceived by others. The outside is visible, and so it often receives the most attention.

Yet every human being possesses an inner life that is just as real. Beneath our words and actions are thoughts, longings, fears, hopes, wounds, and desires. This hidden world shapes much of what we do, even when it remains unseen. Jesus gently reminds us that the One who created the outer life also created the inner life. Neither should be ignored.

Sometimes spirituality itself can become focused on appearances. We may try to look peaceful rather than becoming peaceful. We may try to appear loving rather than allowing love to transform us. We may become concerned with the image of holiness while neglecting the deeper work of honesty, healing, and awakening.

The good news hidden within this saying is that God is not interested only in one part of us. The Divine Presence meets us in our entirety. Our strengths and struggles, our public lives and private thoughts, our visible actions and hidden motivations all belong within the sphere of grace. Nothing within us is outside the reach of God's loving attention.

The contemplative path is not about choosing the inside over the outside. It is about integration. When the inner life is nourished, the outer life begins to reflect it naturally. Compassion becomes visible. Peace becomes tangible. Authenticity replaces performance. The cup becomes whole.

Perhaps the invitation today is simply to spend a few moments attending to your inner life. Not with judgment, but with curiosity and kindness. What is happening beneath the surface? What needs acknowledgment? What longs for healing? The One who fashioned your outward life is already present within your inward life as well. There is no need to hide from that Presence. You are known completely and loved completely.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Search my heart with love*
>
> Exhale: *Make me whole within*
