## 59 - While the Light Is Before You

> *Jesus said, "Take heed of the living one while you are alive, lest you die and seek to see him and be unable to do so."*

This saying carries a sense of urgency, yet it is not the urgency of fear. It is the urgency of presence. Jesus invites his listeners to pay attention to the living reality available to them now rather than postponing their awakening to some distant future.

How often do we assume there will be more time? We tell ourselves that one day we will slow down, become more present, deepen our spiritual lives, or learn to appreciate what truly matters. We imagine that life will eventually provide a better moment for attention, gratitude, and transformation. Yet life is always unfolding in the present tense.

The phrase *"the living one"* points toward a reality that is alive, immediate, and accessible. It is not merely a memory from the past or a hope for the future. The Divine Presence is not waiting for some future stage of our journey before becoming available. It is already here, quietly meeting us in this breath, this moment, this ordinary day.

There is a kind of spiritual sleep that can overtake us. We become distracted by worries, consumed by plans, or trapped in repetitive patterns of thought. Days pass. Seasons pass. We move through life without fully inhabiting it. Then suddenly we realize how much beauty, wisdom, and grace we overlooked while searching elsewhere.

Perhaps the "death" Jesus speaks of is not only a physical event. There are moments when parts of our lives become closed, numb, or disconnected from the living source within us. When awareness fades, we can lose touch with the sacred presence that has been accompanying us all along. The invitation is to awaken before that happens—to become attentive now.

Contemplative practice is one way of accepting this invitation. We pause. We breathe. We listen. We return to the present moment again and again. Not because God is absent, but because our attention often is. We learn to recognize the living one in the silence, in the people around us, in the beauty of creation, and in the depths of our own hearts.

Today, do not wait for a better time to be awake. Notice the life that is already here. Notice the sacred hidden within the ordinary. Let yourself become fully present to this moment. The living one is not far away. The living one is here, waiting to be noticed.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Awaken me now*
>
> Exhale: *To the living presence*
