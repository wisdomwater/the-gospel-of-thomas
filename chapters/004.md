## 4 - Learning from the Beginning

> *Jesus said, "The man old in days will not hesitate to ask a small child seven days old about the place of life, and he will live. For many who are first will become last, and they will become one and the same."*

This saying invites us into a beautiful reversal. We often assume that wisdom belongs to the experienced, the educated, or the aged. Yet here, the elder turns toward the newborn child as a teacher. The one who has lived many years becomes willing to learn from one who has barely entered the world.

Perhaps the image points toward a quality of being rather than an age. A newborn has not yet accumulated the layers of identity, ambition, judgment, and certainty that often gather over a lifetime. There is a freshness there, an immediacy. The child simply is. Present. Open. Uncomplicated.

As we move through life, we gain valuable knowledge, but we can also lose something precious. We become convinced that we already know. We develop opinions about who we are, who others are, and how the world works. These conclusions can become walls that keep us from seeing reality as it is. The spiritual journey often involves not only learning but unlearning.

The elder who asks the child is practicing humility. He is willing to set aside status, expertise, and certainty in order to encounter life anew. There is a kind of wisdom that emerges when we stop trying to be the one with all the answers. In that openness, we become available to the quiet teachings hidden in simple moments, ordinary people, and even our own breath.

The saying concludes with another reversal: the first become last, and they become one and the same. The divisions we carefully construct begin to soften. The successful and the unsuccessful, the wise and the foolish, the teacher and the student—all are seen within a larger wholeness. Life is not a competition for spiritual rank. It is an invitation into deeper unity.

Today, consider where certainty may have become heavy. What if you approached this day with the curiosity of a child and the humility of an elder willing to learn? The place of life may be closer than you think, quietly waiting beneath all that you assume you already know.
