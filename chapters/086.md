## 86 - No Place to Rest

> *Jesus said, "The foxes have their holes and the birds have their nests, but the son of man has no place to lay his head and rest."*

Foxes have their dens. Birds have their nests. Each has a place of shelter, a place to settle, a place that can be called home. Jesus contrasts this with the Son of Man, who has nowhere to lay his head. At first glance, the saying speaks of homelessness and uncertainty. Yet beneath the surface lies a deeper invitation.

We all long for a place to rest. Not merely a physical place, but an inner place of certainty and security. We search for something stable on which to build our identity. We hope that success, relationships, beliefs, possessions, or accomplishments will finally provide a lasting sense of home.

Yet the spiritual journey often reveals that every external refuge is temporary. Circumstances change. Roles shift. Bodies age. Even our most cherished ideas can evolve as we grow. The places where we once rested our identity eventually prove unable to bear the full weight of our longing.

The Son of Man, as a symbol of awakened humanity, may point toward a life that refuses to make its home in anything temporary. Such a life remains free. It moves lightly through the world, grateful for what is given but unwilling to cling. It finds its center not in possessions, status, or certainty, but in a deeper relationship with the Divine.

This freedom can feel unsettling. The ego prefers fixed structures and familiar ground. It wants guarantees. Yet there is a strange peace that emerges when we stop demanding permanence from impermanent things. We begin to discover that our true home is not found in what we hold onto but in the Presence that accompanies us wherever we go.

Perhaps the saying invites us to ask: Where am I seeking rest? What am I relying upon to give me a sense of security and identity? The answer may reveal both our attachments and our opportunities for growth. For when the heart releases its need to build a permanent nest in the passing things of life, it becomes available to a deeper kind of belonging.

The Son of Man has no place to lay his head because the whole of creation has become his dwelling. The awakened heart is at home wherever God is present—and God is present everywhere.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *You are my dwelling place*

> Exhale: *In You I find my rest*
