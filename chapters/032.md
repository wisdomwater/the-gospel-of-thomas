## 32 - Built Upon the Heights

> *Jesus said, "A city being built on a high mountain and fortified cannot fall, nor can it be hidden."*

This saying offers a powerful image of stability and visibility. A city built upon a mountain stands above the surrounding landscape. It cannot easily be shaken, and it cannot be concealed. Its very position reveals it to all who look in its direction.

Many of us spend our lives building upon uncertain ground. We place our trust in circumstances, accomplishments, possessions, or the opinions of others. Yet these foundations are constantly shifting. Success comes and goes. Relationships change. Seasons of life rise and fall. What seems secure one day may feel fragile the next.

Jesus invites us to consider a different kind of foundation. There is a place within us that is higher than our fears and deeper than our changing circumstances. It is the place where truth, love, and divine presence take root. When our lives are built upon that inner foundation, we discover a stability that the storms of life cannot easily destroy.

The saying also reminds us that what is genuine cannot remain hidden forever. We often worry about whether our efforts matter or whether the quiet work of transformation is accomplishing anything. Yet a life grounded in wisdom gradually reveals itself. Compassion becomes visible. Peace becomes noticeable. Integrity shines through ordinary actions. The fruit of the spirit does not need to advertise itself; it naturally becomes apparent.

This visibility is not about seeking attention. A city on a mountain does not strive to be seen. It simply exists where it has been built. In the same way, a person rooted in the sacred does not need to perform spirituality. Their life becomes a quiet witness. Their presence itself communicates something of the peace they have found.

There is comfort in knowing that the deepest work is often hidden before it becomes visible. A city is not built in a day. Stone is laid upon stone. Foundations are carefully prepared. Likewise, the soul is strengthened through countless small acts of faithfulness, awareness, forgiveness, and love. Over time, a resilient inner life begins to emerge.

Today, reflect on the foundation upon which you are building. What supports you when life becomes uncertain? What remains when circumstances change? Return to that deeper ground within. Build there. Trust there. For a life rooted in truth and love becomes like a city upon a mountain—steady in the storm, visible in the darkness, and impossible to hide.
