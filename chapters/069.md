## 69 - The Holy Hunger

> *Jesus said, "Blessed are they who have been persecuted within themselves. It is they who have truly come to know the father. Blessed are the hungry, for the belly of him who desires will be filled."*

This saying turns our attention inward. Rather than speaking of opposition from the outside, Jesus speaks of a struggle within. Most of us know this experience well. We carry conflicting desires, competing voices, and unresolved tensions. Part of us longs for peace while another part clings to fear. Part of us seeks love while another part remains guarded and afraid.

This inner conflict can feel discouraging. We may wonder why the spiritual journey is not simpler. Yet Jesus offers a blessing rather than a condemnation. Perhaps those who have been "persecuted within themselves" are those who have honestly faced the complexity of their own hearts. They have stopped pretending. They have encountered their doubts, wounds, desires, and contradictions with courage.

There is a kind of self-knowledge that can only emerge through this honest encounter. When we stop running from ourselves, we begin to discover what lies beneath the noise. The false certainties loosen. The masks fall away. We become more humble, more compassionate, and more aware of our need for grace. In that vulnerability, we come to know the Father not as an idea, but as a living presence.

Jesus then speaks of hunger. Not all hunger is physical. There is a hunger for meaning, for truth, for belonging, for love. There is a longing that no possession, accomplishment, or distraction can satisfy. Many people spend years trying to silence this hunger, yet it continues to call them toward something deeper.

The beautiful promise of this saying is that such hunger is not a problem to be solved. It is a gift. The longing itself becomes a compass pointing toward the source of life. Those who remain open to that longing, who allow it to deepen rather than numb it, eventually discover that it leads them toward fullness.

The contemplative path teaches us not to fear our hunger. We learn to bring it into prayer, into silence, into awareness. We stop demanding immediate satisfaction and instead allow desire to open us to the Divine. In time, we discover that what we were truly seeking has been seeking us all along.

Today, notice the longings within your heart. Do not rush to fill them with distractions. Sit with them gently. Listen to what they are trying to teach you. Beneath the surface of every holy hunger lies an invitation into deeper life. Trust that the One who planted the longing also knows how to satisfy it.
