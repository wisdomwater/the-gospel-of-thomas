## 108 - Drinking from the Source

> *Jesus said, "He who will drink from my mouth will become like me. I myself shall become he, and the things that are hidden will be revealed to him."*

There is an intimacy in this saying that is difficult to miss. Jesus does not speak of merely listening to words or agreeing with ideas. He speaks of drinking. The image is one of receiving something deeply, allowing it to become part of one's very being. What is offered is not information alone, but a living source that nourishes from within.

Most of us are familiar with the difference between knowing about something and truly experiencing it. We can read about love, yet that is different from being loved. We can discuss peace, yet that is different from resting in peace. In the same way, spiritual wisdom becomes transformative only when it moves beyond the mind and begins to shape the heart.

The promise of becoming "like me" points toward a profound possibility. The goal of the spiritual journey is not simply admiration from a distance. It is participation. The qualities we recognize in Jesus—compassion, freedom, trust, humility, and love—are not meant to remain external ideals. They are seeds that can take root within us and gradually grow into lived reality.

The saying goes even further. "I myself shall become he." Such language speaks of deep union rather than separation. The closer we draw to the divine presence, the less life feels divided into "God over there" and "me over here." We begin to discover that the sacred is not merely something we observe but something we participate in. The life of love becomes our life as well.

As this union deepens, hidden things are revealed. This revelation is not necessarily a collection of secret teachings or hidden knowledge. More often, it is the unveiling of what has always been present. We begin to see ourselves more clearly. We notice patterns we once overlooked. We glimpse the sacred woven through ordinary moments. What was concealed by distraction, fear, or habit slowly comes into view.

The invitation is gentle. We do not need to force understanding. We simply return to the source again and again. We drink deeply from wisdom, compassion, and presence. Over time, what we receive begins to shape us. The boundaries between knowing and being grow thinner. The hidden becomes visible, not because we have mastered a mystery, but because we have allowed ourselves to be transformed by it.

Today, consider what you are drinking from. What words, influences, and experiences are shaping your inner life? The soul becomes like that from which it continually draws nourishment. Choose the waters that lead toward love, and trust the quiet work they perform within you.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Fill me with Your life*
>
> Exhale: *Reveal what is hidden*
