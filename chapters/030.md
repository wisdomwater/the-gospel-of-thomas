## 30 - The Nearness of the One

> *Jesus said, "Where there are three gods, they are gods. Where there are two or one, I am with him."*

This saying arrives with the simplicity of a whisper. It does not explain itself. It leaves space for reflection. Yet within its brief words lies a profound invitation to consider where we seek the sacred and where we discover its presence.

Human beings have always been inclined to multiply their allegiances. We give our devotion to many things: success, approval, security, status, certainty, possessions, and countless other pursuits. Our attention becomes divided among competing voices and desires. The heart can become crowded with many small gods, each demanding its share of our energy.

Yet Jesus turns our attention toward simplicity. "Where there are two or one, I am with him." The saying suggests a movement from fragmentation toward unity. The more scattered we become, the harder it is to recognize the sacred presence already near us. The more gathered we become, the more clearly we begin to see.

There is a deep spiritual truth in this. The divine is often encountered not through complexity but through simplicity. A quiet room. A single prayer. A moment of honest attention. A heart no longer pulled in ten different directions. When the noise subsides, we discover that what we were seeking has been present all along.

The saying may also remind us that sacred presence is not dependent upon crowds, institutions, or external validation. We sometimes imagine that spiritual significance requires large numbers or impressive displays. Yet Jesus speaks of being present even with one. The solitary seeker, the quiet pilgrim, the person sitting alone in prayer is not abandoned. Divine companionship is available in the smallest and most ordinary moments.

There is another beautiful possibility here. When the divided parts of ourselves begin to come together, we become more capable of experiencing that presence. The scattered mind grows still. The conflicted heart finds peace. The many voices within us gradually yield to a deeper center. In that inner unity, we encounter the One who has always been there.

Today, notice where your attention is being pulled. Notice the many demands competing for your devotion. Then gently return to what is essential. Return to this breath. Return to this moment. Return to the quiet center within. You may discover that the sacred is not waiting at the end of a complicated journey. It is already here, faithfully present, closer than you imagined.
