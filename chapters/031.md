## 31 - The Familiar Veil

> *Jesus said, "No prophet is accepted in his own village; no physician heals those who know him."*

There is a quiet sadness woven into this saying. Jesus observes something deeply human: we often struggle to recognize wisdom when it appears too close to home. The familiar can become a veil that prevents us from seeing what is right in front of us.

When we know someone well, we tend to place them inside the stories we have created about them. We remember who they were, what they have done, and how they have behaved in the past. These stories help us navigate life, but they can also limit our vision. We stop seeing the living person before us and instead see our accumulated assumptions.

The same pattern can unfold within ourselves. We become attached to old identities and outdated beliefs about who we are. We tell ourselves that we are incapable of change, unworthy of growth, or destined to repeat familiar patterns. The past becomes a lens through which we interpret every new possibility. In this way, what is familiar can quietly prevent transformation.

The image of the physician is especially powerful. Healing often requires openness. Yet those who believe they already know the healer may resist the very medicine they need. Familiarity can create a subtle arrogance. We assume there is nothing new to learn, nothing deeper to discover, and no fresh insight to receive.

Contemplation invites us to see with beginner's eyes. It asks us to meet each person, each moment, and even ourselves with renewed curiosity. What if the person you think you know is still unfolding? What if wisdom is speaking through an unexpected voice? What if the sacred is trying to reach you through something you have overlooked because it seemed too ordinary?

There is great freedom in releasing our fixed images. When we do, life becomes alive again. The people around us reveal hidden depths. Old experiences take on new meaning. Even familiar teachings can suddenly shine with fresh light. The world becomes less predictable and more mysterious.

Today, consider where familiarity may be limiting your vision. Is there someone you have stopped truly seeing? Is there a possibility within yourself that you have dismissed too quickly? Gently set aside your assumptions and look again. Sometimes the greatest wisdom arrives through the people, places, and truths we thought we already understood.

**Breath Prayer**
