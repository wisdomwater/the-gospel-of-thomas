## 87 - The Freedom to Stand

> *Jesus said, "Wretched is the body that is dependant upon a body, and wretched is the soul that is dependent on these two."*

This saying arrives with stark language, yet beneath it lies an invitation toward freedom. Jesus speaks of dependence upon dependence, of one thing leaning entirely upon another fragile thing. The image calls to mind a house built upon shifting sand, where instability multiplies instability.

Human life is full of necessary relationships. We depend upon one another for care, companionship, wisdom, and love. This saying need not be read as a rejection of connection. Rather, it invites us to examine the difference between healthy relationship and the kind of dependence that causes us to lose ourselves.

Many of us have experienced moments when our peace depended entirely on another person's approval, presence, or behavior. We felt secure only when someone else reassured us. We felt worthy only when someone else affirmed us. In those moments, our inner life became tethered to something outside our control. The soul grows weary when it seeks its foundation in what is constantly changing.

The saying may also point to a deeper pattern. The body depends upon many things—comfort, possessions, status, pleasure, security. When the soul becomes wholly identified with these concerns, it too becomes dependent upon what is temporary. We begin to believe that our well-being rises and falls with circumstances, forgetting the deeper life that remains beneath them.

The contemplative path gently leads us toward a more stable center. It invites us to discover a source of life that is not dependent upon constant validation, perfect conditions, or external success. This deeper center is not isolated or self-sufficient in a rigid sense. Rather, it is rooted in God. From that grounding, we are free to love others without clinging to them and engage the world without being consumed by it.

Perhaps the invitation today is to notice where you are seeking support. What carries the weight of your identity? What determines your sense of peace? There is no shame in discovering places of dependence. Such discoveries are often the beginning of liberation. As your roots sink deeper into the Divine Presence, you may find yourself becoming both more grounded and more free.

The soul was never meant to balance precariously upon passing things. It was created to rest in a love that does not waver, a life that does not fade, and a Presence that remains through every change.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Root me in Your presence*
>
> Exhale: *Free me from what clings*
