## 22 - Making the Two One

> *Jesus saw infants being suckled. He said to his disciples, "These infants being suckled are like those who enter the kingdom." They said to him, "Shall we then, as children, enter the kingdom?" Jesus said to them, "When you make the two one, and when you make the inside like the outside and the outside like the inside, and the above like the below, and when you make the male and the female one and the same, so that the male not be male nor the female; and when you fashion eyes in the place of an eye, and a hand in place of a hand, and a foot in place of a foot, and a likeness in place of a likeness; then will you enter the kingdom."*

This saying begins with a simple image: an infant resting in complete trust. An infant does not yet carry the many divisions that adults accumulate. There is no carefully managed image to protect, no endless comparison, no need to prove worth. The child simply receives life as it comes. Jesus points to this quality as a doorway into the kingdom.

When the disciples ask whether they must become children, Jesus responds with a series of mysterious images about making the two one. Again and again, he speaks of things that seem divided being brought together. Inside and outside. Above and below. Male and female. The language is symbolic, inviting us to consider the many ways we experience separation within ourselves and within the world.

Most of us live with inner divisions. We present one face to others while hiding another from ourselves. We say we value peace while secretly feeding resentment. We long for freedom while clinging to old fears. These divisions create tension within the soul. The kingdom, Jesus suggests, is found not through fragmentation but through integration.

To make the inside like the outside is to become whole. It is the gradual work of aligning what we believe, what we say, and how we live. It is the courage to bring hidden parts of ourselves into the light of awareness and compassion. As we do, the masks become less necessary, and a deeper authenticity begins to emerge.

The image of male and female becoming one can also be seen as a symbol of reconciliation. Human beings often divide reality into opposites and then identify with one side against the other. Yet spiritual maturity invites us beyond rigid oppositions into a larger wholeness. Strength and tenderness, action and receptivity, wisdom and wonder all belong within a fully alive soul.

The kingdom is not merely a destination somewhere else. It is the flowering of unity within us. Every time we heal a division, every time we bring awareness to what has been hidden, every time we choose wholeness over fragmentation, we take a step into that sacred reality. Like the infant in Jesus' image, we begin to rest more deeply in the life that has always held us.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Make me whole*
>
> Exhale: *Gather me into unity*
