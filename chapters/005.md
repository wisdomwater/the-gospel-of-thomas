## 5 - Seeing What Is Already Here

> *Jesus said, "Recognize what is in your sight, and that which is hidden from you will become plain to you. For there is nothing hidden which will not become manifest."*

There is something wonderfully simple about this saying. We often imagine that spiritual insight lies somewhere far away, hidden behind secret knowledge or extraordinary experiences. Yet Jesus points us toward what is already in front of us. Begin with what you can see. Pay attention to what is already present.

Much of life is lived on automatic pilot. We move from task to task, thought to thought, often without truly noticing the world around us. The warmth of a cup in our hands. The sound of wind through leaves. The quiet ache beneath our busyness. The kindness offered by a stranger. Sacred realities are often overlooked not because they are absent, but because we are distracted.

The invitation to recognize what is in our sight is also an invitation to honesty. We cannot awaken to deeper truths while avoiding the truths already available to us. If we ignore our fears, our longings, our habits, or our wounds, we may spend years searching for answers while overlooking the lessons already waiting within our own experience.

Paradoxically, when we become fully present to what is obvious, what is hidden begins to reveal itself. The surface of life becomes transparent to a deeper reality. A simple conversation becomes an opportunity for grace. A difficult season reveals unexpected strength. Ordinary moments begin to shimmer with meaning. Awareness opens doors that striving cannot.

This saying suggests that revelation is often gradual rather than dramatic. We do not force hidden things into the light. We create the conditions for seeing by becoming attentive. Like dawn slowly illuminating a landscape, understanding unfolds as we learn to remain present.

Today, resist the temptation to search somewhere else. Pause and notice what is already before you. Listen carefully. Look closely. Be fully here. The sacred has a way of revealing itself to those who are willing to see what has been present all along.
