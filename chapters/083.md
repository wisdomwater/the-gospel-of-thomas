## 83 - Beyond the Image

> *Jesus said, "The images are manifest to man, but the light in them remains concealed in the image of the light of the father. He will become manifest, but his image will remain concealed by his light."*

We live in a world of images. We see faces, names, roles, appearances, and forms. We encounter people through the stories they tell about themselves and the stories we create about them. Everywhere we look, images present themselves to our awareness.

Yet Jesus points beyond the image to something hidden within it: the light. The image is visible, but the deeper reality it carries often remains concealed. We see the outer form, but we do not always perceive the sacred presence shining through it.

This is true not only of others but also of ourselves. Most of us spend much of our lives identifying with an image. We become attached to our successes and failures, our strengths and weaknesses, our history and our expectations. These things may describe aspects of us, but they are not the deepest truth of who we are. Beneath every image lives a light that cannot be reduced to any label or description.

The saying suggests that as the light becomes manifest, the image becomes less important. This is not because the image is bad, but because the light is greater. When the sun rises, we pay less attention to the lamp. When love fills the heart, we become less preoccupied with maintaining appearances. What matters is no longer how things seem, but what they truly are.

The contemplative journey is often a gradual movement from image to essence. We begin by noticing how much energy is spent protecting, improving, or defending our image. Then, little by little, we discover a deeper identity rooted not in what we project, but in the life of God within us. There we find a freedom that appearances can neither grant nor take away.

Perhaps the invitation today is to look gently beyond the surface. When you encounter another person, ask what light may be hidden within the image before you. When you look at yourself, remember that you are more than the roles you play and the stories you carry. The light may be partially concealed, but it is already present. And as that light shines more clearly, you may discover that what is most real about you has been there all along.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Help me see beyond appearances*
>
> Exhale: *Reveal the light within*
