## 110 - What Cannot Be Possessed

> *Jesus said, "Whoever finds the world and becomes rich, let him renounce the world."*

At first glance, this saying sounds like a contradiction. How can someone find the world, become rich, and then be invited to renounce it? Yet many of life's deepest lessons arrive in the form of paradox. What we think will satisfy us often reveals its limits only after we have obtained it.

Most people spend some part of their lives pursuing what the world offers. Success, recognition, security, achievement, influence, comfort—these are not inherently wrong. They can be gifts, and they often serve important purposes. Yet there comes a moment for many seekers when they realize that even the greatest accomplishments cannot fully satisfy the deepest longing of the heart.

The saying does not necessarily ask us to reject life or despise the world. Rather, it invites us to loosen our grip on the belief that fulfillment can be possessed. We may gain wealth, status, or knowledge, only to discover that peace remains elusive when our identity depends upon them. The problem is not the treasure itself, but our attachment to it.

There is a subtle freedom that emerges when we no longer need the world to tell us who we are. We can enjoy what comes without clinging to it. We can appreciate beauty without trying to own it. We can participate fully in life while remembering that our deepest value does not depend on what we accumulate.

In this sense, renouncing the world is less about withdrawal and more about awakening. It is the release of the illusion that lasting fulfillment can be secured through external means alone. The heart begins to rest in something deeper, something that remains present whether circumstances rise or fall.

Paradoxically, this kind of letting go often allows us to engage the world more fully. Freed from the need to possess, prove, or protect, we become available to love. We discover gratitude where there was striving and generosity where there was grasping. Life becomes a gift rather than a possession.

Today, notice what you may be holding too tightly. Is there something you believe you must have in order to be complete? Gently bring it into the light of awareness. The invitation is not to reject the blessings of life, but to discover the freedom that comes when your deepest treasure is found within rather than without.
