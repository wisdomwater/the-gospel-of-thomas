## 24 - The Light Within

> *His disciples said to him, "Show us the place where you are, since it is necessary for us to seek it." He said to them, "Whoever has ears, let him hear. There is light within a man of light, and he lights up the whole world. If he does not shine, he is darkness."*

The disciples ask a familiar question. They want to know where to find Jesus. They are looking for a place, a destination, a location that can be sought and reached. It is the same impulse that lives within many of us. We imagine that the sacred is somewhere else—waiting in a distant future, hidden in a special place, or reserved for extraordinary moments.

Yet Jesus answers in an unexpected way. He does not point to a mountain, a temple, or a distant kingdom. Instead, he points inward. He speaks of a light already present within the one who is able to see it. The place they seek is not merely somewhere to travel. It is something to awaken to.

Most of us know what it feels like to lose touch with that inner light. We become consumed by worry, resentment, fear, distraction, or exhaustion. The light is not gone, but it can become obscured, like the sun hidden behind thick clouds. We begin to live from habit rather than awareness, from reaction rather than presence.

The contemplative path is, in many ways, the gentle uncovering of that light. Through silence, prayer, attention, and compassion, we begin to notice the sacred presence already shining within us. We discover that divine life is not something we must manufacture. It is something we learn to recognize and express.

Jesus says that the one who possesses this light "lights up the whole world." This does not mean becoming famous or extraordinary. A person rooted in presence changes the atmosphere around them. Their kindness becomes a refuge. Their peace becomes contagious. Their compassion creates space for others to breathe. The light naturally shares itself.

The saying ends with a challenge as well as an invitation. If the light remains hidden, darkness prevails—not because darkness is stronger, but because the light was never allowed to shine. The world does not need perfection from us. It needs authenticity. It needs people willing to live from the deepest truth they have discovered.

Today, rather than searching for the sacred somewhere far away, pause and turn your attention inward. Beneath the noise, beneath the fears, beneath the endless activity of the mind, a light is already present. Tend it. Trust it. Let it shine through your words, your actions, and your presence. In doing so, you may discover that the place you were seeking has been within you all along.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Light of God within me*
>
> Exhale: *Shine through my life*
