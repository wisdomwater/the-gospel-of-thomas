## 73 - The Ripened Field

> *Jesus said, "The harvest is great but the laborers are few. Beseech the Lord, therefore, to send out laborers to the harvest."*

Jesus paints a simple image: a field ready for harvest. The grain is ripe. The season has arrived. The abundance is already present. Yet there are too few hands to gather what is waiting.

We often imagine spiritual growth as something distant, something that will happen someday after enough effort or enough understanding. Yet this saying suggests that much is already ready. The field is not barren. The harvest is not future. The sacred is already present in the lives around us, within us, and all through ordinary moments. The challenge is not that God is absent. The challenge is that so few are awake enough to notice.

There are countless opportunities each day to participate in this harvest. A word of encouragement. A moment of listening. A gesture of kindness. A choice to respond with patience instead of irritation. Most spiritual labor does not look dramatic. It often appears as simple acts of presence that allow love to move through us into the world.

The saying can also be heard inwardly. Within every person is a field waiting to be gathered. Insights arise. Invitations emerge. Seeds planted long ago begin to bear fruit. Yet how often do we rush past them? How often do we overlook the quiet work of grace unfolding beneath the surface of our lives? There is a harvest within us that longs to be received.

Jesus does not tell his listeners to manufacture the harvest. He tells them to pray for laborers. This is a subtle but important distinction. We are not asked to force growth or create abundance through our own striving. Instead, we are invited to become willing participants in something already happening. The Divine is constantly at work, and the prayer is that more hearts become available to join that work.

Perhaps today the invitation is simply to pay attention. Look at the people around you. Look at your own life. Notice where compassion is waiting to be expressed, where wisdom is waiting to be embodied, where love is waiting to be shared. The field may be far more ripe than you realize.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Open my eyes to the harvest*
>
> Exhale: *Make me willing to serve*
