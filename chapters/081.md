## 81 - The Crown Laid Down

> *Jesus said, "Let him who has grown rich be king, and let him who possesses power renounce it."*

This saying presents a surprising contrast. The first half seems to affirm authority, while the second calls for its surrender. Together they create a paradox that invites us to look beyond the usual meanings of wealth and power.

When we hear the word "rich," we often think of possessions, status, or success. Yet there are other forms of wealth. A person may be rich in wisdom, rich in compassion, rich in patience, or rich in the quiet knowledge of God's presence. Such richness does not diminish when shared. In fact, it grows.

If someone has become truly rich in this way, perhaps they are ready to be king—not as a ruler over others, but as one who governs their own heart. They are no longer driven by every impulse, fear, or desire. They have discovered an inner stability that allows them to move through life with grace and freedom.

The second half of the saying turns our expectations upside down. Those who possess power are told to renounce it. This is not necessarily a rejection of responsibility or influence. Rather, it points toward a deeper freedom. Power has a subtle tendency to cling to the self. It whispers that control will bring security, that authority will bring significance, that dominance will bring peace.

Yet the spiritual path often moves in the opposite direction. The deeper we grow, the less need we feel to control. We become less concerned with winning, proving, managing, or possessing. We begin to trust. We learn that love does not need to dominate and that wisdom does not need to force itself upon others.

This renunciation is not a loss but a liberation. When we release our grip on power, our hands become free to receive something greater. We discover the strength of humility, the authority of compassion, and the quiet influence of a life rooted in truth.

Perhaps the invitation today is to consider what kind of riches you are cultivating and what forms of power you are still holding tightly. The Kingdom often grows where generosity replaces possession and trust replaces control. There is a crown that comes not from grasping, but from letting go.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Teach me true richness*
>
> Exhale: *Help me release control*
