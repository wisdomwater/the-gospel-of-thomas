## 43 - Beyond Dividing

> *His disciples said to him, "Who are you, that you should say these things to us?"*
>
> *Jesus said to them, "You do not realize who I am from what I say to you, but you have become like those who love the tree and hate its fruit, or love the fruit and hate the tree."*

The disciples ask a question that lives within every spiritual journey: *Who are you?* Yet beneath it may lie another question as well: *How can I fit you into what I already understand?* We often encounter life this way. We long for certainty, for clear categories, for something we can define and hold. Yet some realities are too living, too vast, to be contained by our concepts.

Jesus responds with an image of division. Some love the tree but reject its fruit. Others enjoy the fruit but disregard the tree that gave it life. The image points toward a habit deeply rooted within us: the tendency to separate what belongs together. We prefer one side of a mystery and push away the other. We embrace what comforts us and resist what challenges us.

This pattern appears throughout ordinary life. We want growth without discomfort. We seek wisdom without uncertainty. We desire transformation without surrender. We celebrate the blossoms while ignoring the roots. Yet wholeness asks us to welcome the entire living reality, not merely the portions we find agreeable.

The spiritual path often involves healing these inner divisions. We begin to see that the visible and invisible, the outer and inner, the journey and the destination, are not enemies. Like tree and fruit, they belong to one another. The fruit reveals the tree. The tree makes the fruit possible. Each tells the story of the other.

Perhaps Jesus is gently inviting his listeners to see with deeper eyes. Instead of asking, "Which part should I choose?" we might ask, "How are these connected?" Wisdom often emerges not from taking sides but from perceiving the hidden unity beneath apparent opposites. What once seemed divided begins to reveal itself as one living whole.

Today, notice where you may be resisting part of your own experience. Notice where you love the fruit but resent the roots, or cling to the tree while overlooking its gift. Allow yourself to rest in a wider vision. Beneath the divisions of the mind, life is already weaving everything together.
