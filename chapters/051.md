## 51 - Already Here

> *His disciples said to him, "When will the repose of the dead come about, and when will the new world come?"*
>
> *He said to them, "What you look forward to has already come, but you do not recognize it."*

The disciples ask a question that has echoed through every generation: *When will it happen?* When will peace arrive? When will the world be made new? When will healing finally come? We often imagine fulfillment waiting somewhere ahead, just beyond the horizon of the present moment.

Jesus responds in a startling way. He does not point to a future date or describe a coming event. Instead, he suggests that what they seek is already present. The problem is not that it has not arrived. The problem is that it has not been recognized.

How often do we live this way? We postpone joy until circumstances improve. We delay gratitude until life becomes easier. We imagine that peace will come after the next accomplishment, the next breakthrough, or the next season of life. Yet while our attention is fixed on tomorrow, we can miss the sacred reality quietly unfolding today.

This does not mean that all things are complete or that suffering is unreal. The world still groans with longing. Hearts still ache. Yet beneath the unfinished nature of life, there is already a deeper reality available to us. The Kingdom is present even amid imperfection. The new world begins wherever awareness awakens and love is allowed to flourish.

Contemplation is, in many ways, the practice of recognition. It teaches us to notice what has always been here. The breath moving through us. The presence surrounding us. The grace hidden within ordinary moments. The life of God quietly shining through creation. We are not bringing the sacred into existence. We are learning to see it.

Perhaps the repose of the dead is not only a future hope but also a present invitation. The weary parts of us can rest now. The anxious striving that constantly reaches for "someday" can soften now. Something new is already emerging whenever we awaken to the life that is before us.

Today, instead of asking what is missing, pause and ask what is already here. Sit quietly for a moment. Listen. Look. Breathe. The gift you have been awaiting may be closer than you imagined, hidden not in the future but in the wonder of this very moment.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Open my eyes*
>
> Exhale: *To what is here*
