## 9 - Preparing the Soil of the Heart

> *Jesus said, "Now the sower went out, took a handful (of seeds), and scattered them. Some fell on the road; the birds came and gathered them up. Others fell on the rock, did not take root in the soil, and did not produce ears. And others fell on thorns; they choked the seed(s) and worms ate them. And others fell on the good soil and it produced good fruit: it bore sixty per measure and a hundred and twenty per measure."*

This familiar image begins with remarkable generosity. The sower scatters seeds everywhere. There is no careful sorting, no attempt to place each seed in the perfect location. The seeds are simply given. Life itself often feels this way. Wisdom, grace, love, beauty, and insight are continually being offered to us, scattered across the landscape of our days.

Yet the same seed produces different results depending on where it lands. The story gently shifts our attention away from the seed and toward the soil. The question is not whether life is offering opportunities for growth. The question is whether our hearts are prepared to receive them.

There are times when the soil within us becomes hardened like a well-traveled road. We move through life quickly, distracted and preoccupied. Moments of beauty or truth touch us briefly but never sink beneath the surface. The seed is present, but there is no space for it to take root.

At other times, the seed finds shallow ground. We receive inspiration with enthusiasm, but when difficulties arise, the roots have nowhere to grow. Or perhaps the thorns of worry, resentment, ambition, and endless busyness begin to crowd our inner world. What was planted is slowly choked by competing demands for our attention.

Yet Jesus also speaks of good soil. Good soil is not perfect soil. It is simply receptive. It is open enough to receive what is given and patient enough to nurture what begins to grow. The contemplative life is, in many ways, the gradual cultivation of this inner receptivity. Through silence, honesty, compassion, and awareness, the heart becomes a place where deeper life can flourish.

The promise of the saying is one of abundance. Even a single seed can bear fruit beyond expectation when it finds a welcoming home. Today, rather than striving to produce more, perhaps the invitation is simply to soften the soil. Make room. Listen. Receive. Trust that the seeds already scattered throughout your life are quietly carrying more possibility than you can yet imagine.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Soften my heart*
>
> Exhale: *Let life take root*
