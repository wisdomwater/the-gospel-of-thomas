## 67 - Nothing Missing

> *Jesus said, "If one who knows the all still feels a personal deficiency, he is completely deficient."*

This saying arrives as a challenge to one of the deepest habits of the human mind: the belief that something essential is missing. We often move through life with a quiet sense of incompleteness. If only we were wiser, more successful, more spiritual, more loved, more certain—then we would finally be enough.

Jesus points toward a different possibility. He speaks of one who "knows the all" and yet continues to feel deficient. The paradox is striking. How can someone who has encountered the fullness of reality still believe they are lacking? Perhaps the deficiency is not in what they possess, but in how they see.

Many spiritual traditions speak of a fundamental wholeness beneath the surface of life. We may lose touch with it. We may forget it. We may become entangled in fear, comparison, and striving. Yet the deeper truth remains unchanged. The ocean does not cease to be the ocean because a wave imagines itself separate.

This does not mean that we stop growing. There will always be things to learn, wounds to heal, and ways to mature. The spiritual journey is real. But growth is very different from deficiency. Growth begins from the recognition that we are already rooted in something whole. Deficiency begins from the belief that our value must be earned.

Much of our suffering comes from trying to fill an emptiness that exists largely in the imagination. We seek completion through achievements, possessions, approval, or spiritual accomplishments. Yet no amount of accumulation can satisfy a sense of lack that is based on forgetting who we are.

The contemplative path gently returns us to remembrance. In silence, we begin to notice that beneath all our thoughts of inadequacy there is a deeper presence. A deeper life. A deeper belonging. We discover that we are held within a reality far larger than our fears. The heart begins to relax because it no longer has to prove its worth.

Today, notice where the feeling of "not enough" arises within you. Do not fight it. Simply observe it with compassion. Then look deeper. Beneath the voice of deficiency is the quiet truth of your being. You are already part of the whole. You already belong. Nothing essential is missing.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *I belong to the Whole*
>
> Exhale: *Nothing is missing*
