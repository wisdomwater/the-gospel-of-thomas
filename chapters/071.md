## 71 - What Cannot Be Rebuilt

> *Jesus said, "I shall destroy this house, and no one will be able to build it [...]."*

At first hearing, these words sound unsettling. We tend to cherish what is familiar. We build identities, beliefs, routines, and expectations that help us feel secure in a changing world. The thought of a house being destroyed can awaken fear, resistance, or grief. Yet spiritual transformation often begins precisely where our certainty begins to crack.

There are houses we construct within ourselves that were never meant to be permanent. We build them from assumptions about who we are, what we must achieve, how others should see us, or what we believe will finally make us whole. Over time, these inner structures can become so familiar that we mistake them for our true selves. We forget that the house is not the one who lives within it.

The spiritual journey often involves a gentle dismantling. Sometimes life itself becomes the instrument. A disappointment, a loss, an unexpected change, or a moment of awakening can reveal that what we trusted is not as solid as we imagined. The walls begin to loosen. The old framework no longer fits. What once seemed essential begins to fall away.

Yet perhaps the saying points toward something deeper than destruction alone. When a false house collapses, it cannot simply be rebuilt in its previous form. Once we have seen with new eyes, we cannot entirely return to old ways of seeing. Awareness changes us. Love changes us. The heart that has tasted a larger freedom cannot comfortably shrink back into its former confines.

This can feel disorienting for a time. We may long for the comfort of the familiar structure. But beneath the falling stones, something more enduring remains. What is real does not need to be defended. What is true does not depend on walls. The Divine Presence has never been confined to any house we build in the mind.

Perhaps the invitation today is not to fear what is crumbling, but to trust what remains. The sacred life within you is older than every identity and deeper than every story. When the temporary falls away, you may discover that the One you have been seeking was never housed there at all.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *What is false may fall away*
>
> Exhale: *What is real remains*
