## 48 - When the Inner House Becomes One

> *Jesus said, "If two make peace with each other in this one house, they will say to the mountain, 'Move Away,' and it will move away."*

This saying begins not with a mountain, but with peace. Before anything moves in the outer world, something is reconciled within the house. Jesus points our attention toward a hidden reality: great obstacles are often connected to divisions that remain unresolved beneath the surface.

Most of us know what it feels like to live with inner conflict. One part of us wants to trust while another clings to fear. One part longs to forgive while another holds tightly to old wounds. We desire change, yet we resist it. We move through life carrying competing voices within the same heart. The house becomes divided, and our energy is scattered.

When peace begins to emerge between these divided parts, something remarkable happens. We no longer spend our strength fighting ourselves. The energy once consumed by inner conflict becomes available for clarity, compassion, and purposeful action. We become more whole. Our thoughts, intentions, and actions begin to move in the same direction.

The mountain in this saying may represent any obstacle that seems immovable. A habit that has followed us for years. A fear that continually returns. A wound that shapes how we see ourselves and others. Such mountains often appear permanent when we are fragmented within. Yet when reconciliation takes place in the inner house, what once seemed impossible can begin to shift.

This peace does not come through force. We do not conquer parts of ourselves or drive them away. Rather, we learn to listen deeply. We welcome what has been hidden. We allow fear, grief, anger, and hope to sit at the same table. In the light of awareness and love, enemies become companions, and division gives way to understanding.

Perhaps Jesus is reminding us that true power flows from wholeness. The heart at peace carries a quiet strength that does not need to struggle for control. When we become inwardly united, we discover that many mountains are not as immovable as they once appeared.

Today, notice any places of tension within your own house. Instead of choosing sides, become a peacemaker. Listen with compassion to the conflicting voices within. Let them rest together in the presence of Divine Love. You may find that as peace grows within, the mountains before you begin to move.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Bring peace within me*
>
> Exhale: *Make my heart one*
