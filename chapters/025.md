## 25 - As Your Own Soul

> *Jesus said, "Love your brother like your soul, guard him like the pupil of your eye."*

This saying is remarkably simple. There are no mysteries to unravel, no paradoxes to contemplate, no hidden symbols to decode. Jesus speaks directly of love. Yet beneath these few words lies a depth that could occupy a lifetime of reflection.

Most of us find it easier to love in theory than in practice. We readily extend kindness to those who agree with us, support us, or reflect our values. But life continually places before us people who challenge our patience, frustrate our expectations, or reveal parts of ourselves we would rather not see. It is here that love becomes more than a feeling. It becomes a way of being.

Jesus does not merely say to love your brother. He says to love him as your own soul. This is a profound invitation. We naturally care for our own wounds, protect our own well-being, and seek our own happiness. To love another as our own soul is to recognize that their joy and suffering are not entirely separate from our own. It is to see beyond the illusion of isolation.

The image of guarding another like the pupil of your eye deepens this teaching. The eye instinctively protects itself. The slightest threat causes it to blink. We do not have to remind ourselves to guard it; the response is immediate and natural. Jesus invites us toward a love that becomes equally attentive—a love that notices, cares, and protects without calculation.

This kind of love does not require us to lose ourselves. In fact, it often begins with learning to see ourselves rightly. As we become more compassionate toward our own fears, wounds, and imperfections, we often find it easier to extend that same compassion outward. The walls between self and other begin to soften. We discover that mercy given and mercy received flow from the same source.

Perhaps this is one of the great secrets of the spiritual life: the more deeply we encounter the divine presence within ourselves, the more naturally we recognize it in others. Every person becomes worthy of care. Every encounter becomes sacred ground. Love ceases to be an occasional act and becomes the atmosphere in which we live.

Today, consider someone who needs your patience, your understanding, or your kindness. See them not as a problem to solve or an obstacle to overcome, but as a soul deserving the same care you would offer your own. In doing so, you may find that the love you give returns to you, deepening your own heart in the process.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Teach me to love*
>
> Exhale: *As one soul among many*
