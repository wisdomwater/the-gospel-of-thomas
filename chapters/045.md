## 45 - The Fruit of the Heart

> *Jesus said, "Grapes are not harvested from thorns, nor are figs gathered from thistles, for they do not produce fruit. A good man brings forth good from his storehouse; an evil man brings forth evil things from his evil storehouse, which is in his heart, and says evil things. For out of the abundance of the heart he brings forth evil things."*

Jesus begins with a simple observation from nature. We do not search for grapes among thorns or figs among thistles. The fruit we encounter reveals something about the plant from which it grows. In the same way, our words, actions, and responses reveal something of what is taking root within us.

Most of us spend a great deal of energy trying to manage appearances. We attempt to improve our behavior, choose better words, or hide the parts of ourselves we find troubling. While these efforts have value, Jesus gently directs our attention deeper. The real question is not simply what appears on the surface. The real question is: What is filling the heart from which these things arise?

When irritation, judgment, or fear spill out of us, it can be tempting to condemn ourselves. Yet moments like these can become invitations rather than failures. They reveal what is asking to be healed. The fruit points us toward the root. Instead of reacting with shame, we can become curious. What wound, belief, fear, or attachment is asking for attention beneath the surface?

The beautiful news hidden within this saying is that hearts can change. Gardens can be cultivated. What is planted today influences the harvest of tomorrow. Every act of kindness, every moment of forgiveness, every return to presence, every choice to dwell in love becomes a seed quietly growing within us. Over time, the inner storehouse begins to fill with different treasures.

Contemplative practice teaches us that transformation happens from the inside out. As we learn to rest in Divine Love, we gradually discover that our responses begin to change. Compassion arises more naturally. Patience grows deeper roots. We become less reactive and more present. The fruit changes because the soil itself is being renewed.

Today, pay attention not only to the fruit but to the garden of your heart. Notice what you have been feeding with your attention. Notice what you have been watering with your thoughts. Then gently choose what you wish to cultivate. The harvest may not appear overnight, but every seed of love planted within you carries the promise of abundant fruit.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Plant love within me*
>
> Exhale: *Let good fruit grow*
