## 93 - Honoring the Pearl

> *Jesus said, "Do not give what is holy to dogs, lest they throw them on the dung-heap. Do not throw the pearls to swine, lest they [...] it [...]."*

At first hearing, this saying can sound harsh. Yet beneath its imagery lies a gentle invitation toward wisdom and discernment. Jesus speaks of holy things and precious pearls—images that suggest something sacred, valuable, and easily overlooked by those who are not ready to receive them.

Most of us have experienced the pain of sharing something deeply meaningful only to have it dismissed or misunderstood. A tender truth, a personal insight, a moment of vulnerability, or a sacred experience can feel diminished when offered into a space that is unable or unwilling to receive it. Not every gift belongs in every setting.

The spiritual journey teaches us that timing matters. Seeds planted in frozen ground do not take root. Wisdom forced upon another rarely becomes wisdom at all. Love does not demand acceptance. It waits patiently for openness. Discernment is not withholding out of superiority; it is honoring the nature of what is being given.

This saying may also apply inwardly. Within each of us are pearls of insight that emerge through prayer, silence, suffering, and awakening. Yet we can sometimes treat these gifts carelessly. We rush past them. We distract ourselves from them. We trade them for lesser concerns. The soul knows when it has touched something holy, but the mind often hurries on to the next thing.

The pearl may be a moment of stillness, a glimpse of grace, a deeper awareness of God's presence, or a truth about ourselves that arrives unexpectedly. Such treasures deserve our attention. They are not meant to be displayed for approval or immediately translated into certainty. Sometimes they are meant simply to be treasured, pondered, and allowed to mature within us.

Perhaps the invitation today is to ask: What pearl has been placed in my hands? What sacred thing in my life needs care rather than display, patience rather than explanation? Not every truth needs to be spoken immediately. Not every insight needs to be defended. Some gifts are best honored by holding them gently until the right moment arrives.

The holy is not fragile because it is weak. It is precious because it is deep. And what is deep often asks for reverence before it asks to be shared.

**Breath Prayer**

> Inhale: *Help me honor what is sacred*
>
> Exhale: *Teach me wise discernment*
